# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Easy Dinners Everyone Will Eat

Your whole week of dinners, planned. Five crowd-pleasing meals that work for busy weeknights, with flavors everyone at the table will enjoy. Here's what's on the menu.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Honey Garlic Chicken Thighs | 35 min |
| Tuesday | Cheesy Beef and Rice Skillet | 25 min |
| Wednesday | Baked Salmon with Roasted Vegetables | 30 min |
| Thursday | Spaghetti with Meat Sauce | 20 min |
| Friday | Chicken and Vegetable Quesadillas | 25 min |

---

## Monday: Honey Garlic Chicken Thighs

Start the week with this sweet and savory crowd-pleaser. The honey glaze caramelizes beautifully in the oven while you prep tomorrow's lunch.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 8 bone-in chicken thighs
- 1/3 cup honey
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Green onions for garnish

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Pat chicken thighs dry and season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken thighs skin-side down for 5 minutes until golden.
3. Flip chicken and cook 3 more minutes. Remove chicken to a plate.
4. In the same skillet, whisk together honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, rice vinegar, and red pepper flakes.
5. Return chicken to skillet, skin-side up. Transfer to oven and bake 15-20 minutes until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
6. Garnish with sliced green onions before serving.

## Tuesday: Cheesy Beef and Rice Skillet

Everything cooks in one pan for this comforting Tuesday night dinner. Kids love the melted cheese, adults appreciate how simple cleanup is.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 cup white rice, uncooked
- 2 cups beef broth
- 1 can diced tomatoes, drained
- 1 cup frozen corn
- 1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. In a large skillet, brown ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks, about 6 minutes.
2. Add diced onion and cook until softened, 3-4 minutes. Drain excess fat.
3. Stir in rice, beef broth, diced tomatoes, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 15 minutes until rice is tender.
5. Stir in frozen corn and 1 cup of cheese. Cook until cheese melts, about 2 minutes.
6. Top with remaining cheese and let melt before serving.

## Wednesday: Baked Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

Mid-week reset with this healthy, colorful dinner. Everything roasts together on one sheet pan while you help with homework.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 2 cups baby carrots
- 1 zucchini, sliced
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 2 teaspoons dried dill
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Lemon wedges for serving

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Toss broccoli, carrots, and zucchini with 2 tablespoons olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on one side of the sheet pan.
3. Place salmon fillets on the other side of the pan. Drizzle with remaining olive oil and lemon juice.
4. Season salmon with dill, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
5. Roast for 15-20 minutes until salmon flakes easily and vegetables are tender.
6. Serve with lemon wedges.

## Thursday: Spaghetti with Meat Sauce

The fastest dinner of the week. While the pasta boils, the sauce simmers, and dinner is ready before anyone gets too hungry.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 12 oz spaghetti
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 1 jar marinara sauce (24 oz)
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1/2 teaspoon oregano
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Parmesan cheese for serving

### Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions.
2. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add diced onion and cook 3 minutes.
3. Add ground turkey and cook, breaking it up, until browned, about 6-8 minutes.
4. Add minced garlic, Italian seasoning, and oregano. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Stir in marinara sauce and simmer 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
6. Drain pasta and serve topped with meat sauce and Parmesan cheese.

## Friday: Chicken and Vegetable Quesadillas

End the week with these crispy, cheesy quesadillas. Perfect for using up leftover chicken, and everyone can customize their own.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
- 8 flour tortillas
- 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1 small onion, diced
- 1 cup spinach, chopped
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Sour cream and salsa for serving

### Instructions
1. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté bell pepper and onion until softened, 4-5 minutes.
2. Add spinach and cook until wilted, 1 minute. Remove vegetables from pan.
3. In a bowl, combine shredded chicken with cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
4. Place filling on half of each tortilla: chicken, sautéed vegetables, and cheese. Fold tortillas in half.
5. Heat remaining oil in the skillet. Cook quesadillas 2-3 minutes per side until golden and cheese melts.
6. Cut into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa.
