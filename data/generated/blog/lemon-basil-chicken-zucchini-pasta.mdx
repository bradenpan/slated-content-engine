# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Light Spring Dinners Under 30 Minutes

Spring is here, and your dinner table should celebrate it. Five fresh, light meals that take advantage of the season's best ingredients — all ready in 30 minutes or less. Here's what your family is eating this week.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus | 20 min |
| Tuesday | Mediterranean Chicken Orzo Skillet | 28 min |
| Wednesday | Spring Vegetable Stir-Fry with Shrimp | 18 min |
| Thursday | Herb-Crusted Pork Tenderloin with Spring Salad | 28 min |
| Friday | Lemon Basil Chicken and Zucchini Pasta | 23 min |

---

## Monday: Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus

Start the week with bright, clean flavors. The salmon cooks quickly, and the asparagus gets perfectly tender in the same pan with garlic and lemon butter.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 lb asparagus, trimmed
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Season salmon with salt, pepper, and thyme. Cook skin-side up 4 minutes, then flip and cook 3-4 minutes more.
3. Remove salmon and tent with foil. Add remaining oil and asparagus to pan. Cook 3 minutes.
4. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds. Add lemon juice, zest, and butter. Toss asparagus to coat.
5. Return salmon to pan briefly to warm through. Serve immediately.

---

## Tuesday: Mediterranean Chicken Orzo Skillet

Everything cooks in one skillet — chicken, pasta, vegetables, and all. The orzo absorbs the flavors as it cooks, and the feta melts into creamy pockets throughout.

**Prep time:** 8 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast, cubed
- 1 cup orzo pasta
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 red onion, diced
- 1/2 cup kalamata olives, sliced
- 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and 1 teaspoon oregano. Cook until golden, 5-6 minutes.
3. Add onion and cook 2 minutes. Add orzo, stirring to coat with oil.
4. Add broth, cherry tomatoes, sun-dried tomatoes, olives, and remaining oregano. Bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat, cover and simmer 12-15 minutes until orzo is tender and liquid is absorbed.
6. Stir in feta and parsley. Drizzle with remaining olive oil before serving.

## Wednesday: Spring Vegetable Stir-Fry with Shrimp

The fastest dinner of the week. Crisp vegetables, tender shrimp, and a light sauce that doesn't mask the fresh flavors. Perfect over rice or on its own.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 8 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cups snap peas, trimmed
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup baby carrots, halved lengthwise
- 3 green onions, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch
- Cooked rice for serving

### Instructions
1. Toss shrimp with cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat.
2. Cook shrimp 2 minutes per side until pink. Remove and set aside.
3. Add remaining oil to pan. Stir-fry carrots 2 minutes, then add bell pepper and snap peas. Cook 2 minutes more.
4. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Return shrimp to pan. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Toss everything together 1 minute.
6. Garnish with green onions and serve over rice.

## Thursday: Herb-Crusted Pork Tenderloin with Spring Salad

A slightly more elegant meal for Thursday night. The pork gets a quick sear then roasts while you assemble the fresh strawberry and pecan salad.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 18 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 2 pork tenderloins (1 lb each)
- 6 cups mixed spring greens
- 1 cup strawberries, sliced
- 1/4 cup pecans, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon honey
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425F. Mix rosemary, thyme, 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper.
2. Rub herb mixture all over pork tenderloins. Heat an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Sear tenderloins on all sides, about 6 minutes total. Transfer skillet to oven.
4. Roast 12-15 minutes until internal temperature reaches 145F. Rest 5 minutes, then slice.
5. Whisk together remaining olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon, and honey for dressing.
6. Toss greens with dressing, top with strawberries and pecans. Serve with sliced pork.

## Friday: Lemon Basil Chicken and Zucchini Pasta

End the week with this bright, herb-forward pasta. The zucchini adds freshness without weighing down the dish, and everything comes together in one pan.

**Prep time:** 8 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, sliced thin
- 12 oz linguine pasta
- 2 medium zucchini, spiralized or julienned
- 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1/4 cup white wine or chicken broth
- 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
- 2 tablespoons butter
- Red pepper flakes to taste
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Cook linguine according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Cook until golden and cooked through, 6-7 minutes.
4. Add garlic and red pepper flakes, cook 30 seconds. Add wine and lemon juice, simmer 2 minutes.
5. Add cooked pasta, zucchini, and remaining olive oil. Toss with pasta water as needed.
6. Remove from heat, stir in butter, lemon zest, basil, and Parmesan. Serve immediately.
