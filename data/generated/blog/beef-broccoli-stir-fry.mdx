# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Dinners Everyone Will Eat

Your whole week of dinners, planned around one simple truth: everyone at your table will actually eat these meals. Five recipes that work for picky eaters and busy parents, with flavors that please the whole family.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Crispy Baked Chicken Drumsticks | 45 min |
| Tuesday | Simple Spaghetti and Meatballs | 40 min |
| Wednesday | Sheet Pan Pork Chops with Sweet Potatoes | 35 min |
| Thursday | Chicken Quesadillas | 25 min |
| Friday | Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry | 25 min |

---

## Monday: Crispy Baked Chicken Drumsticks

Start the week with crispy chicken that bakes hands-free while you handle homework and backpacks. The drumsticks get golden and crunchy, the potatoes roast alongside, and everyone gets exactly what they want.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 35 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 8 chicken drumsticks
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 lbs baby potatoes, halved

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Mix panko, Parmesan, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper in a shallow dish.
3. Pat chicken drumsticks dry and brush with olive oil. Roll in breadcrumb mixture to coat.
4. Toss halved potatoes with remaining olive oil and place on sheet pan with chicken.
5. Bake 35 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and potatoes are golden and tender.

---

## Tuesday: Simple Spaghetti and Meatballs

The dinner that never fails. Homemade meatballs that actually taste homemade, but come together in under an hour on a Tuesday night.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes | **Cook time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
- 1 egg
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
- 12 oz spaghetti

### Instructions
1. Mix ground beef, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Form into 16 meatballs.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Brown meatballs on all sides, about 8 minutes total.
3. Add marinara sauce, bring to a simmer. Cover and cook 15 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, cook spaghetti according to package directions. Drain.
5. Serve meatballs and sauce over spaghetti.

## Wednesday: Sheet Pan Pork Chops with Sweet Potatoes

Mid-week comfort food that roasts on one pan. The sweet potatoes get caramelized, the pork stays juicy, and you get a complete meal with minimal cleanup.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 bone-in pork chops (1 inch thick)
- 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Mix brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
3. Rub pork chops with 1 tablespoon olive oil and season with half the spice mixture.
4. Toss sweet potatoes and onion with remaining oil and spice mixture.
5. Arrange pork chops and vegetables on sheet pan. Bake 25 minutes until pork reaches 145°F.

## Thursday: Chicken Quesadillas

The fastest dinner of the week. Crispy tortillas filled with seasoned chicken and melted cheese, ready in 25 minutes. Serve with whatever sides you have on hand.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
- 8 flour tortillas (8 inch)
- 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1/4 cup diced onion
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Sour cream and salsa for serving

### Instructions
1. Mix shredded chicken with cumin and chili powder.
2. Place filling on half of each tortilla: chicken, cheese, bell pepper, and onion.
3. Fold tortillas in half and press gently.
4. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
5. Cook quesadillas 2-3 minutes per side until golden and cheese melts. Repeat with remaining quesadillas.
6. Cut into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa.

---

## Friday: Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

End the week with takeout flavors made at home. The sauce is sweet and savory, the beef stays tender, and even the kids will eat the broccoli when it tastes like this.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes | **Cook time:** 10 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb beef sirloin, sliced thin
- 4 cups broccoli florets
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 2 tablespoons water
- 4 cups cooked white rice

### Instructions
1. Mix soy sauce, brown sugar, cornstarch, and water in a small bowl.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat.
3. Add beef and stir-fry 3-4 minutes until browned. Remove to a plate.
4. Add remaining oil, then broccoli. Stir-fry 2 minutes until bright green.
5. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry 30 seconds. Return beef to pan.
6. Pour in sauce and stir-fry 1-2 minutes until thickened. Serve over rice.
