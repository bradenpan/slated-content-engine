# Instant Pot Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Bowl - One-Pot Dinner

This is the kind of dinner that saves Tuesday nights. Everything cooks together in one pot, the rice comes out perfectly fluffy, and cleanup is basically nonexistent. Plus, it's naturally gluten-free without any special ingredients or substitutions.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True one-pot meal.** Protein, starch, and vegetables all cook together with zero babysitting.
- **Naturally gluten-free.** No weird substitutions or specialty ingredients required.
- **Kid-approved.** Mild flavors and familiar textures that even picky eaters will eat.
- **Scales easily.** Double it for meal prep or leftovers that actually taste good reheated.
- **Foolproof rice.** The Instant Pot delivers perfect rice every single time.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1.5 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

## Instructions

1. Set Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add olive oil and heat until shimmering.
2. Add diced onion and cook 3-4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add chicken pieces, thyme, paprika, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Stir to coat and cook 3-4 minutes until chicken is lightly browned.
4. Add rinsed rice and chicken broth. Stir once to combine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
5. Add frozen vegetables on top without stirring. Secure the lid and set valve to sealing.
6. Pressure cook on high for 10 minutes, then natural release for 10 minutes before quick-releasing remaining pressure.
7. Carefully remove lid and fluff rice with a fork. Let stand 5 minutes before serving.
8. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Boneless chicken breast works too (use 1.5 lbs), or try turkey thighs for a different flavor. Ground turkey or chicken also work—just brown it first and break it up.

**Rice alternatives:** Brown rice needs 22 minutes cooking time and 3 cups of broth. Quinoa works with 1 cup quinoa to 2 cups broth and 8 minutes cooking time.

**Vegetable options:** Fresh broccoli florets, diced bell peppers, or sliced carrots all work. Add them with the frozen vegetables in step 5.

**Make it dairy-free:** This recipe is already dairy-free, making it perfect for multiple dietary restrictions at once.

**Spice it up:** Add a pinch of red pepper flakes with the other seasonings, or stir in a tablespoon of your favorite hot sauce after cooking.

**Meal prep friendly:** Portion into containers and refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat with a splash of broth to restore moisture.
