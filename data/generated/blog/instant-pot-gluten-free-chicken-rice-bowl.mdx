# Easy Instant Pot Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Bowl

This is the kind of dinner that saves Tuesday nights. Everything cooks together in one pot, the rice comes out perfectly fluffy, and there's actual protein and vegetables in every bite. Your gluten-free family members get a complete meal, and everyone else just gets a really good dinner.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True one-pot meal.** Chicken, rice, and vegetables all cook together with zero babysitting.
- **Naturally gluten-free.** No special ingredients or substitutions needed.
- **Kid-approved.** Mild flavors that work for picky eaters, but tasty enough for adults.
- **Minimal cleanup.** One pot, one spoon, done.
- **Scales easily.** Double it for meal prep or leftovers that actually taste good reheated.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1.5 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: fresh parsley for garnish

## Instructions

1. Set Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add olive oil and heat until shimmering.
2. Season chicken pieces with salt, pepper, thyme, paprika, and garlic powder. Add to pot and cook 5-6 minutes until lightly browned.
3. Add diced onion and garlic. Cook 2-3 minutes until onion softens.
4. Add rinsed rice and stir to coat with oil and seasonings, about 1 minute.
5. Pour in chicken broth and add frozen vegetables. Stir gently to combine.
6. Secure lid, set valve to sealing, and pressure cook on high for 10 minutes.
7. When cooking is complete, let pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick release remaining pressure.
8. Fluff rice with a fork, taste and adjust seasoning. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Boneless chicken breast works, but thighs stay more tender. Turkey thighs or pork shoulder cubes are great alternatives.

**Different vegetables:** Fresh broccoli florets, diced bell peppers, or chopped carrots work just as well as frozen mixed vegetables.

**Make it dairy-free:** This recipe is already dairy-free, but you can stir in a splash of coconut milk at the end for extra richness.

**Spice it up:** Add a pinch of red pepper flakes with the other seasonings, or stir in hot sauce after cooking.

**Meal prep friendly:** Portion into containers and refrigerate up to 4 days. Reheat with a splash of broth to restore moisture.

**Rice alternatives:** Substitute with quinoa (use same liquid ratio) or cauliflower rice (add in the last 2 minutes of natural release).
