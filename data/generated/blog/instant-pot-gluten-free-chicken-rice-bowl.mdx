# Instant Pot Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Bowl - 30 Minutes

This is the dinner that saves Tuesday nights when everyone's hungry and you need something that actually fills people up. Everything cooks together in one pot, the rice comes out perfectly fluffy, and even the pickiest eaters go back for seconds.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True one-pot meal.** Chicken, rice, and vegetables all cook together with zero babysitting.
- **Naturally gluten-free.** No weird substitutions or specialty ingredients required.
- **Kid-approved.** Familiar flavors that don't scare anyone away from the dinner table.
- **Minimal cleanup.** One pot, one spoon, done.
- **Scales easily.** Double it for meal prep or halve it for smaller families.
- **Pantry-friendly.** Uses ingredients you probably already have on hand.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1.5 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons gluten-free tamari or soy sauce
- 1 cup shredded cheese, optional

## Instructions

1. Press sauté on the Instant Pot and heat olive oil. Add diced onion and cook 3 minutes until softened.
2. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add chicken pieces, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chicken is lightly browned.
4. Add rinsed rice and stir to coat with the oil and seasonings.
5. Pour in chicken broth and tamari. Stir once to combine.
6. Add frozen vegetables on top without stirring.
7. Secure the lid, set valve to sealing, and pressure cook on high for 10 minutes.
8. When cooking is complete, let pressure naturally release for 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
9. Stir everything together and let sit 5 minutes to absorb any remaining liquid. Top with cheese if using.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Boneless chicken breast works but will be less tender. Cut cooking time to 8 minutes. Ground turkey or beef also work - brown first, then add rice and liquid.

**Different vegetables:** Fresh broccoli florets, diced bell peppers, or corn all work. Add fresh vegetables with the rice, frozen ones on top.

**Make it dairy-free:** Skip the cheese or use a dairy-free alternative. The dish is plenty flavorful without it.

**Rice alternatives:** Brown rice needs 22 minutes cook time and an extra 1/2 cup liquid. Quinoa works with 8 minutes cook time.

**Spice it up:** Add a diced jalapeño with the onion or a pinch of red pepper flakes with the seasonings.

**Meal prep friendly:** Stores in the fridge for 4 days. Reheat with a splash of broth to loosen it up.
