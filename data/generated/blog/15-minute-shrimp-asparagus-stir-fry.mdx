# 15-Minute Shrimp and Asparagus Stir Fry

This is what happens when you need dinner in a hurry but still want something that feels like you actually cooked. Fifteen minutes from start to finish, one pan, and everyone at the table gets vegetables without complaining about it.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 15 minutes.** No prep work beyond trimming asparagus and mincing garlic.
- **Shrimp cooks fast.** Two minutes per side and you're done. No waiting around.
- **Asparagus stays crisp.** Quick cooking keeps it bright green and snappy.
- **Simple sauce.** Three ingredients that pack serious flavor without fuss.
- **One pan cleanup.** Everything cooks in the same skillet.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lbs medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced thin
- 2 green onions, sliced
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Pat shrimp dry and season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat.
3. Add shrimp in a single layer. Cook 1-2 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
4. Add remaining oil to the same pan. Add asparagus and bell pepper, stir fry 2-3 minutes until crisp-tender.
5. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil.
7. Return shrimp to the pan, add sauce mixture, and toss everything together for 1 minute.
8. Remove from heat, sprinkle with green onions, and serve immediately over rice or noodles.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Use chicken breast (sliced thin, cook 3-4 minutes per side) or scallops (2 minutes per side). Tofu works too - cube it, press out moisture, and pan-fry until golden.

**Different vegetables:** Broccoli florets, snap peas, or sliced zucchini all work. Keep the cooking time short to maintain crunch.

**Make it spicier:** Add a pinch of red pepper flakes with the garlic, or drizzle with sriracha before serving.

**Prep shortcuts:** Buy pre-cooked shrimp and just toss them in during the last minute to heat through. Use pre-trimmed asparagus from the grocery store.

**Make ahead:** Prep all vegetables and mix the sauce the night before. Store everything separately in the fridge, then it's just assembly and cooking.

**Serve with:** Jasmine rice, brown rice, or rice noodles. Even works over quinoa if that's your thing.
