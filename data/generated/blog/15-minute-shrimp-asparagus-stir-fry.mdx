# 15-Minute Shrimp and Asparagus Stir Fry

This is what happens when you need dinner in fifteen minutes and actually want to eat something good. Shrimp cooks fast, asparagus stays crisp, and the whole thing comes together in one pan before anyone can ask "what's for dinner" twice.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 15 minutes.** Shrimp cooks in under 2 minutes per side, asparagus needs just 3 minutes to stay crisp.
- **One pan, minimal cleanup.** Everything happens in the same skillet.
- **Kid-friendly flavors.** The sauce is savory, not spicy, and most kids will eat the shrimp.
- **Flexible vegetables.** Swap asparagus for broccoli, snap peas, or bell peppers.
- **Scales easily.** Double it for meal prep or leftovers tomorrow.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lbs medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes, optional
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds for garnish

## Instructions

1. Pat shrimp dry and season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat.
3. Add shrimp in a single layer. Cook 1-2 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
4. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the same pan. Add asparagus and stir-fry 2-3 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender.
5. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes.
7. Return shrimp to the pan, pour sauce over everything, and toss for 1 minute until heated through.
8. Remove from heat, sprinkle with green onions and sesame seeds, and serve immediately over rice.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Chicken breast works great - slice thin and cook 3-4 minutes per side. Tofu cubes need about 4-5 minutes to get golden.

**Different vegetables:** Broccoli florets, snap peas, bell peppers, or mushrooms all work. Adjust cooking time - harder vegetables need an extra minute or two.

**Make it spicier:** Double the red pepper flakes or add a tablespoon of sriracha to the sauce mixture.

**Prep shortcuts:** Buy pre-cooked shrimp and just toss it in during the last minute to heat through. Pre-trimmed asparagus saves a few minutes too.

**Sauce variations:** Skip the oyster sauce and add an extra tablespoon of soy sauce if that's what you have. A squeeze of lime juice at the end brightens everything up.

**Make ahead:** Prep all your vegetables and make the sauce earlier in the day. When it's time to cook, everything moves fast.
