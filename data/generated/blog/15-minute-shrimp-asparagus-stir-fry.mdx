# 15-Minute Shrimp and Asparagus Stir Fry

This is what happens when you need dinner fast and actually want to eat something good. Fifteen minutes from start to finish, one pan, and everyone's happy. The asparagus stays crisp, the shrimp doesn't get rubbery, and the sauce brings it all together.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 15 minutes.** No prep-ahead required, no lengthy ingredient lists.
- **One pan cleanup.** Your wok or large skillet handles everything.
- **Shrimp cooks fast.** Perfect for those nights when you forgot to defrost anything else.
- **Asparagus stays crisp.** Quick cooking keeps the vegetables bright and snappy.
- **Scales up easily.** Double it for meal prep or a bigger crowd.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. Pat shrimp dry and season with salt and pepper. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch.
2. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until shimmering.
3. Add shrimp in a single layer. Cook 1-2 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
4. Add asparagus to the same pan. Stir fry 2-3 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender.
5. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. Return shrimp to the pan, pour in sauce mixture, and toss everything together for 1 minute until sauce thickens.
7. Remove from heat, drizzle with sesame oil, and garnish with green onions and sesame seeds.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Chicken breast (sliced thin) or scallops work great. Cook chicken 3-4 minutes per side, scallops 1-2 minutes per side.

**Different vegetables:** Broccoli, snap peas, or bell peppers all cook in the same time frame. Cut everything roughly the same size.

**Make it spicier:** Add a teaspoon of chili garlic sauce or red pepper flakes with the garlic and ginger.

**Frozen shrimp shortcut:** Keep a bag of frozen shrimp in the freezer. Run under cold water for 5 minutes to thaw while you prep everything else.

**Serve over:** Rice, noodles, or quinoa. Or just eat it straight from the pan with chopsticks.

**Make ahead:** The sauce can be whisked together and stored in the fridge for up to a week. Everything else cooks so fast, there's no point in prepping ahead.
