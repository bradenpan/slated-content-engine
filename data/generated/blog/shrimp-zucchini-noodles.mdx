# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Light Spring Dinners Under 30 Minutes

Spring is here, and your dinner routine is ready for a refresh. Five light, fresh meals that celebrate the season without keeping you in the kitchen all evening. Every recipe clocks in under 30 minutes, and together they make one complete week.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus | 20 min |
| Tuesday | Mediterranean Chicken Orzo | 28 min |
| Wednesday | Honey Soy Pork Tenderloin with Sugar Snap Peas | 28 min |
| Thursday | Caprese Stuffed Chicken | 30 min |
| Friday | Shrimp and Zucchini Noodles | 18 min |

---

## Monday: Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus

Start the week with the simplest dinner in the rotation. Everything goes on one pan, and the bright lemon flavor feels like spring on a plate.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Toss asparagus with 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange on one side of the pan.
3. Season salmon with salt and pepper. Place on the other side of the pan.
4. Mix remaining olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, zest, and dill. Brush over salmon.
5. Roast 12-15 minutes until salmon flakes easily and asparagus is tender-crisp.

---

## Tuesday: Mediterranean Chicken Orzo

A one-skillet dinner that brings together all the flavors of the Mediterranean. The orzo cooks right in the pan, so there's minimal cleanup.

**Prep time:** 8 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts, diced
- 1½ cups orzo pasta
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- ½ cup kalamata olives, pitted and halved
- ½ cup feta cheese, crumbled
- ¼ cup red onion, diced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and oregano.
2. Cook chicken 5-6 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove to a plate.
3. Add orzo to the same skillet. Toast for 1 minute, then add chicken broth.
4. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer 10-12 minutes until orzo is tender.
5. Stir in chicken, tomatoes, olives, and red onion. Cook 2 minutes until heated through.
6. Remove from heat and top with feta cheese before serving.

## Wednesday: Honey Soy Pork Tenderloin with Sugar Snap Peas

Mid-week calls for something a little special but still quick. The honey soy glaze makes this feel restaurant-worthy without the fuss.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 18 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 2 pork tenderloins (1 lb each)
- 1 lb sugar snap peas, trimmed
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Season pork with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Sear pork on all sides until golden, about 6 minutes total.
4. Transfer skillet to oven and roast 12-15 minutes until internal temperature reaches 145°F.
5. Meanwhile, whisk honey, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl.
6. Heat remaining oil in a large pan. Stir-fry snap peas 3-4 minutes until bright green.
7. Slice pork and serve with snap peas, drizzled with honey soy sauce and sprinkled with sesame seeds.

## Thursday: Caprese Stuffed Chicken

Thursday deserves something that looks impressive but doesn't require chef skills. The classic caprese combination never disappoints.

**Prep time:** 12 minutes | **Cook time:** 18 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
- 4 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
- 2 large tomatoes, sliced
- ¼ cup fresh basil leaves
- 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Toothpicks for securing

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Cut a pocket in each chicken breast, being careful not to cut all the way through.
2. Season chicken inside and out with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.
3. Stuff each pocket with mozzarella, tomato slices, and basil. Secure with toothpicks.
4. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
5. Sear chicken 3-4 minutes per side until golden.
6. Transfer to oven and bake 12-15 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.
7. Let rest 5 minutes, remove toothpicks, and drizzle with balsamic glaze before serving.

## Friday: Shrimp and Zucchini Noodles

End the week with the fastest dinner on the plan. Light, fresh, and ready in under 20 minutes — perfect for Friday night.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 8 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1½ lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 medium zucchini, spiralized
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- ¼ cup white wine or chicken broth
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 lemon, juiced and zested
- ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
- Red pepper flakes to taste
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Season shrimp with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Cook shrimp 2-3 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove to a plate.
4. Add butter and garlic to the same skillet. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add wine and lemon juice. Simmer 1 minute.
6. Add zucchini noodles and toss 2-3 minutes until just tender.
7. Return shrimp to pan, add lemon zest and parsley. Toss and serve immediately.
