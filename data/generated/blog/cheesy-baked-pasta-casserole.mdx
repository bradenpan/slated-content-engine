This is a Tuesday night dinner. Pasta, meat sauce, three cheeses, baked until bubbly — the kind of meal your kids will ask for by name and your whole family will actually finish. It takes about 45 minutes, uses one skillet and one baking dish, and the leftovers are genuinely good the next day.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Realistic 45 minutes,** including bake time. You're not rushing.
- **Kid-approved by design.** Familiar flavors, cheesy top, nothing suspicious-looking on the plate.
- **One skillet, one baking dish.** Cleanup is manageable on a weeknight.
- **Built-in flexibility.** Easy to sneak in vegetables, swap the protein, or make it ahead.
- **Feeds a crowd.** Six solid servings — enough for dinner and tomorrow's lunch.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb penne or rotini pasta
- 1 lb lean ground beef or ground turkey
- 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
- 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
- 1 cup whole milk ricotta cheese
- 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Optional: 1 cup finely diced zucchini or spinach, stirred into the sauce

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish.
2. Cook pasta in salted boiling water for 2 minutes less than the package directions — it will finish cooking in the oven. Drain and set aside.
3. Brown ground beef or turkey in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks, about 6-7 minutes. Drain excess fat.
4. Add marinara sauce, diced tomatoes, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and onion powder to the skillet. Stir to combine and simmer 3-4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
5. In a large bowl, combine the drained pasta, meat sauce, ricotta, 1 cup of the mozzarella, and 1/4 cup of the Parmesan. Stir until everything is evenly coated.
6. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer. Top with the remaining 1 cup mozzarella and 1/4 cup Parmesan.
7. Cover tightly with foil and bake 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown at the edges.
8. Let the casserole rest 5 minutes before serving — this helps it hold together when you scoop it.

## Tips and Substitutions

- **Swap the protein:** Ground turkey works just as well as beef and cooks the same way. Italian sausage (casings removed) adds more flavor if your kids are into it. For a meatless version, a can of white beans stirred into the sauce holds up well.
- **Hide the vegetables:** Finely diced zucchini, a handful of spinach, or shredded carrots disappear into the sauce once it simmers. The cheese on top does the rest of the work.
- **Make it ahead:** Assemble the whole casserole the night before, cover with foil, and refrigerate. Pull it out when you get home, add 5-10 minutes to the covered bake time since it's going in cold.
- **Dairy-free swap:** Use a dairy-free ricotta and shredded mozzarella — the texture is slightly different but it still bakes up well. Skip the Parmesan or use a dairy-free version.
- **Leftovers:** Reheat individual portions with a splash of water and a loose cover in the microwave — keeps the pasta from drying out. It reheats well, though the cheese layer softens a bit. Still good. Still gone.
