# Creamy Tuscan Chicken Pasta — Ready in 25 Minutes

This is the kind of dinner that makes Tuesday feel like a celebration. Creamy, satisfying, and packed with flavor from sun-dried tomatoes and fresh spinach — all ready before the kids start asking "when's dinner" for the third time.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 25 minutes** from start to finish. Cook the pasta while you prep everything else.
- **One skillet for the sauce** means minimal cleanup on a busy weeknight.
- **Kid-friendly but grown-up flavors.** The cream sauce makes vegetables disappear while adults get their sun-dried tomato fix.
- **Looks impressive** but uses pantry staples and rotisserie shortcuts if needed.
- **Doubles easily** for meal prep or when the neighbors drop by unexpectedly.

## Ingredients

- 12 oz penne or rigatoni pasta
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes in oil, chopped
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 3 cups fresh baby spinach
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: fresh basil for garnish

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water, then drain.
2. While pasta cooks, season chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add chicken to skillet and cook 5-6 minutes until golden brown and cooked through. Remove chicken and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Pour in heavy cream and chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
6. Stir in Parmesan cheese and Italian seasoning until cheese melts and sauce is smooth.
7. Add cooked pasta, chicken, and spinach to the skillet. Toss until spinach wilts and everything is well coated. Add pasta water if needed to thin sauce.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh basil if desired.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Use rotisserie chicken:** Skip steps 2-3 and add 3 cups of shredded rotisserie chicken in step 7. Cuts the cooking time to 15 minutes.

**Make it lighter:** Swap half the heavy cream for half-and-half or whole milk. The sauce will be thinner but still creamy.

**Different vegetables:** Frozen peas, sliced mushrooms, or chopped bell peppers work great. Add heartier vegetables with the garlic, softer ones with the spinach.

**Dairy-free version:** Use coconut cream and nutritional yeast instead of heavy cream and Parmesan. Add a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness.

**Prep ahead:** Chop the chicken and sun-dried tomatoes the night before. Store in the fridge and dinner comes together even faster.

**Leftover magic:** This reheats beautifully with a splash of chicken broth to loosen the sauce. Perfect for tomorrow's lunch.
