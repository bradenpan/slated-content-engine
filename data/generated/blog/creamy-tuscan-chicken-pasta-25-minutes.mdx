# Creamy Tuscan Chicken Pasta — Ready in 25 Minutes

This is the kind of dinner that makes Tuesday feel like a celebration. Rich, creamy sauce with tender chicken and just enough vegetables to feel good about it — all done in one pan while the pasta cooks.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 25 minutes** from start to finish. The pasta and chicken cook at the same time.
- **One skillet finish.** Everything comes together in the same pan you cooked the chicken in.
- **Kid-approved vegetables.** Spinach wilts right into the creamy sauce, and sun-dried tomatoes add sweetness.
- **Restaurant flavor at home.** That rich, creamy sauce tastes way more complicated than it is.
- **Flexible protein.** Works with chicken thighs, shrimp, or even leftover rotisserie chicken.

## Ingredients

- 12 oz penne or rigatoni pasta
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- 3/4 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 3 cups fresh baby spinach
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water, then drain.
2. While pasta cooks, season chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add chicken to skillet and cook 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and cooked through. Remove chicken and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Pour in heavy cream and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer and cook 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
6. Stir in Parmesan cheese and Italian seasoning until melted and smooth.
7. Add cooked pasta, chicken, and spinach to the skillet. Toss everything together until spinach wilts and pasta is well coated.
8. If sauce seems too thick, add reserved pasta water 1-2 tablespoons at a time until desired consistency is reached.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Use chicken thighs for extra flavor, or add cooked shrimp in the last 2 minutes. Leftover rotisserie chicken works too — just add it with the spinach to warm through.

**Different vegetables:** Swap spinach for chopped kale or broccoli florets. Add them with the cream so they have time to cook through.

**Make it lighter:** Use half-and-half instead of heavy cream, or substitute Greek yogurt stirred in at the very end (off the heat to prevent curdling).

**Prep ahead:** Cut the chicken and chop the sun-dried tomatoes the night before. Store in the fridge and this becomes a true 15-minute dinner.

**Double it:** This recipe scales perfectly for meal prep or feeding a crowd. Use a large Dutch oven instead of a skillet.
