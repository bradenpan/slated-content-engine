# Creamy Tuscan Chicken Pasta — Ready in 25 Minutes

This is the kind of dinner that makes Tuesday night feel special without any extra work. Rich, creamy sauce with tender chicken and vegetables that disappear into every bite — even the kids ask for seconds.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 25 minutes** from start to finish, including boiling the pasta water.
- **One-pan sauce** while the pasta cooks — no juggling multiple dishes.
- **Restaurant vibes at home** with ingredients you probably already have.
- **Sneaks in vegetables** with spinach that wilts right into the creamy sauce.
- **Leftovers reheat perfectly** for tomorrow's lunch (add a splash of milk to loosen the sauce).

## Ingredients

- 1 lb penne or rigatoni pasta
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- 3 cups fresh baby spinach
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: fresh basil for garnish

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.

2. While pasta cooks, season chicken pieces with salt and pepper.

3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook 5-6 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove chicken and set aside.

4. In the same skillet, add garlic and sun-dried tomatoes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.

5. Pour in heavy cream and chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.

6. Stir in Parmesan cheese and Italian seasoning until melted and smooth.

7. Add cooked chicken back to the pan along with the spinach. Stir until spinach wilts.

8. Add drained pasta to the skillet and toss to coat. If sauce seems too thick, add reserved pasta water 1 tablespoon at a time.

9. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh basil if desired.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Shrimp works beautifully — cook 2-3 minutes per side until pink. Turkey or pork tenderloin cut into strips also work well.

**Different vegetables:** Swap spinach for chopped kale, broccoli florets, or sliced zucchini. Add heartier vegetables with the garlic to give them more cooking time.

**Make it lighter:** Use half-and-half instead of heavy cream, or substitute Greek yogurt stirred in at the very end (don't let it boil).

**Dairy-free version:** Use coconut cream and nutritional yeast instead of heavy cream and Parmesan. The sun-dried tomatoes still give tons of flavor.

**Prep ahead:** Cut the chicken and prep all ingredients the night before. The actual cooking still takes just 15 minutes.

**Pasta shape matters:** Short, sturdy shapes like penne, rigatoni, or shells hold the creamy sauce better than long noodles.
