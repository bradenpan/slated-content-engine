# Hidden Veggie Chicken Parmesan — Kid-Approved Recipe

This is the chicken parmesan that finally got our pickiest eater to stop asking "what's for dinner" with that worried look. The vegetables are completely invisible, blended right into the marinara sauce, and the crispy chicken keeps everyone happy.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Vegetables disappear completely.** Blended smooth into the sauce — no chunks, no texture issues, no complaints.
- **Crispy coating stays crispy.** Pan-searing first, then finishing in the oven keeps the breadcrumbs golden and crunchy.
- **One skillet dinner.** Less cleanup when you're already negotiating with a six-year-old about eating vegetables.
- **Sneaky nutrition boost.** A full serving of vegetables per person, hidden in plain sight.
- **Familiar flavors.** Tastes like the chicken parmesan they already love, just better for them.

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to 1/2 inch thick
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
- 1 medium zucchini, roughly chopped
- 1 cup baby spinach
- 1 medium carrot, roughly chopped
- 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Set up breading station: flour in one dish, beaten eggs in another, and panko mixed with 1/4 cup Parmesan in a third.
3. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Dredge each piece in flour, then egg, then breadcrumb mixture, pressing to adhere.
4. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown.
5. While chicken cooks, blend marinara sauce, zucchini, spinach, and carrot in a blender until completely smooth, about 60 seconds.
6. Pour the veggie-blended sauce over the chicken in the skillet.
7. Top each chicken breast with mozzarella and remaining Parmesan cheese.
8. Transfer skillet to oven and bake 12-15 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and cheese is bubbly and golden.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Different vegetables:** Yellow squash, bell peppers, or cauliflower work just as well. Keep the total amount around 2 cups of chopped vegetables.

**Make it gluten-free:** Swap regular flour for almond flour and use gluten-free breadcrumbs. The rest of the recipe stays exactly the same.

**Prep ahead:** Pound and bread the chicken in the morning. Store covered in the fridge until dinner time.

**No oven-safe skillet:** Transfer the seared chicken to a baking dish, pour the sauce over, add cheese, and bake as directed.

**Extra sneaky:** Add a handful of white beans to the sauce blend for protein and fiber. They disappear completely and add creaminess.

**Leftover sauce:** This veggie-packed marinara is perfect for pasta night later in the week. Make a double batch and store half in the fridge.
