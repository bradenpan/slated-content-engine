# Hidden Veggie Chicken Parmesan — Kid-Approved Recipe

This is the chicken parmesan that finally got our pickiest eater to stop asking "what's for dinner" with that worried look. The vegetables disappear completely into the sauce, so all they taste is the familiar flavors they already love.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Vegetables vanish completely** when blended into the sauce — no chunks, no texture changes
- **Familiar flavors** kids already know and love: crispy chicken, melted cheese, marinara
- **One skillet** from start to finish means less cleanup on a busy weeknight
- **Extra nutrition** without the dinner table negotiations
- **Freezer-friendly** — make a double batch and freeze half for next month

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to 1/2 inch thickness
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1/2 cup flour
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 jar marinara sauce (24 oz)
- 1 cup finely chopped carrots
- 1 cup finely chopped zucchini
- 1/2 cup finely chopped bell pepper
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Set up breading station: flour in one dish, beaten eggs in another, panko mixed with parmesan in a third.
3. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Dredge each piece in flour, then egg, then breadcrumb mixture.
4. Cook chicken in hot oil 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
5. In the same skillet, sauté carrots, zucchini, and bell pepper for 5 minutes until softened.
6. Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Pour in marinara sauce and simmer 3 minutes.
7. Use an immersion blender to blend the sauce until smooth, or transfer to a regular blender.
8. Return chicken to the skillet, spooning sauce over top. Sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.
9. Bake 12-15 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and cheese is bubbly and golden.

## Tips and Substitutions

- **Different vegetables:** Cauliflower, yellow squash, or spinach work just as well. Keep the total amount at 2.5 cups chopped.
- **Make it dairy-free:** Use nutritional yeast instead of parmesan in the breading and dairy-free mozzarella on top.
- **Prep ahead:** Bread the chicken in the morning and refrigerate. The vegetables can be chopped and stored separately.
- **Protein swap:** This works with pork chops or even thick fish fillets — adjust cooking time accordingly.
- **Extra sneaky:** Add a handful of spinach to the sauce before blending. It won't change the color but adds iron and folate.
- **Freeze for later:** Assemble completely, cover tightly, and freeze up to 3 months. Bake from frozen, adding 10-15 extra minutes.
