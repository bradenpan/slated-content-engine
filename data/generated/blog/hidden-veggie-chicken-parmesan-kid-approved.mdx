# Hidden Veggie Chicken Parmesan — Kid-Approved Recipe

This is the chicken parmesan that finally got our vegetable-resistant kids on board. The secret is blending cauliflower and zucchini right into the marinara sauce — they taste the familiar cheese and crispy chicken, while you know they're getting a full serving of vegetables.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Vegetables are invisible.** Blended cauliflower and zucchini disappear into the sauce completely.
- **Familiar flavors win.** Crispy chicken, melted cheese, and tomato sauce — nothing scary here.
- **One-pan dinner.** Brown the chicken, add sauce and cheese, bake. Done in 35 minutes.
- **Meal prep friendly.** Make extra sauce and freeze it for next time.
- **Scales easily.** Double the recipe for leftovers or a bigger family.

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to 1/2-inch thickness
- 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese, plus extra for serving
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 cup marinara sauce
- 1/2 cup steamed cauliflower florets
- 1/2 cup grated zucchini, excess water squeezed out
- 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Set up breading station with flour in one dish, beaten eggs in another, and panko mixed with parmesan, Italian seasoning, and garlic powder in a third.
2. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Dredge each piece in flour, then egg, then breadcrumb mixture, pressing to adhere.
3. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown.
4. While chicken cooks, blend marinara sauce, steamed cauliflower, and squeezed zucchini in a blender until smooth.
5. Pour the veggie-marinara sauce over the browned chicken in the skillet.
6. Top each chicken breast with mozzarella cheese and transfer skillet to oven.
7. Bake 12-15 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and cheese is bubbly and golden.
8. Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Sprinkle with extra parmesan if desired.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Make it gluten-free:** Swap regular flour for almond flour and use gluten-free breadcrumbs.

**Different hidden veggies:** Pureed carrots, butternut squash, or yellow bell peppers work just as well. Steam first, then blend.

**Prep the sauce ahead:** Make a double batch of the veggie-marinara sauce and freeze half for next time. It keeps for 3 months.

**Swap the protein:** This works with pork chops or even thick fish fillets. Adjust cooking time as needed.

**Make it dairy-free:** Use nutritional yeast instead of parmesan and dairy-free mozzarella for the topping.

**Serve with pasta:** Toss some cooked pasta with any leftover sauce for a complete meal.
