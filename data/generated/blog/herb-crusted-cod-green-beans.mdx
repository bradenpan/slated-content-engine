# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Light Spring Dinners Under 30 Minutes

Spring is here, and your dinner routine is getting a fresh start. Five light, bright meals that celebrate the season without keeping you in the kitchen past 30 minutes. Here's what's on the menu this week.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus | 20 min |
| Tuesday | Mediterranean Chicken Bowls | 25 min |
| Wednesday | Spring Pea and Mint Pasta | 17 min |
| Thursday | Asian Lettuce Wraps with Turkey | 18 min |
| Friday | Herb-Crusted Cod with Green Beans | 25 min |

---

## Monday: Lemon Garlic Salmon with Asparagus

Start the week with this bright, restaurant-quality meal that comes together on one pan. The asparagus gets perfectly tender while the salmon stays flaky and moist.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 lb asparagus, trimmed
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 lemon, juiced and zested
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried dill
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Toss asparagus with 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange on one side of the pan.
3. Mix remaining olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, zest, and dill. Season salmon with salt and pepper, then brush with lemon mixture.
4. Place salmon on the pan with asparagus. Bake 12-15 minutes until salmon flakes easily and asparagus is tender.

## Tuesday: Mediterranean Chicken Bowls

All the fresh flavors of the Mediterranean in a bowl that feels like vacation. The chicken cooks quickly while you prep the fresh vegetables.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into strips
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 cucumber, diced
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup kalamata olives, pitted
- 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Season chicken strips with salt, pepper, and oregano. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Cook chicken strips 6-8 minutes until golden and cooked through. Remove from heat.
3. In a large bowl, combine tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and olives. Toss with remaining olive oil and vinegar.
4. Divide vegetable mixture into bowls. Top with warm chicken and crumbled feta.

## Wednesday: Spring Pea and Mint Pasta

The fastest dinner of the week, and maybe the most cheerful. Bright green peas and fresh herbs make this pasta feel like spring on a plate.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 12 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 12 oz penne pasta
- 2 cups frozen peas
- 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
- 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 lemon, zested and juiced
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Cook pasta according to package directions. Add frozen peas to the pot during the last 2 minutes of cooking.
2. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Drain pasta and peas, reserving 1/2 cup pasta water. Add pasta mixture to the skillet.
4. Toss with lemon juice, zest, herbs, and Parmesan. Add pasta water as needed for creaminess. Season with salt and pepper.

## Thursday: Asian Lettuce Wraps with Turkey

Light, fresh, and fun to eat. Ground turkey keeps these wraps lean while the crisp vegetables add perfect crunch.

**Prep time:** 8 minutes | **Cook time:** 10 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 1 head butter lettuce, leaves separated
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 cup shredded carrots
- 3 green onions, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sriracha (optional)

### Instructions
1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook ground turkey, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes.
2. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Stir in bell pepper and carrots. Cook 2-3 minutes until vegetables are crisp-tender.
4. Mix in soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sriracha. Remove from heat and stir in green onions.
5. Serve turkey mixture in lettuce cups.

## Friday: Herb-Crusted Cod with Green Beans

End the week with this elegant but simple fish dinner. The herb crust gives the mild cod lots of flavor while the green beans roast alongside.

**Prep time:** 7 minutes | **Cook time:** 18 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 cod fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
- 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 1 lemon, cut into wedges
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss green beans with 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a sheet pan.
2. Roast green beans 8 minutes while preparing fish.
3. Mix panko, herbs, garlic, remaining olive oil, salt, and pepper. Press mixture onto cod fillets.
4. Move green beans to one side of pan. Add cod fillets to the other side. Bake 10-12 minutes until fish flakes easily.
5. Serve with lemon wedges.
