## Monday: Honey Garlic Salmon with Roasted Broccoli

Salmon is one of those proteins that looks impressive but actually asks very little of you. The glaze here — honey, garlic, soy sauce, a little sesame oil — comes together in about 90 seconds, and everything roasts on one pan. The broccoli goes on the same sheet, so cleanup is minimal.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

The one thing that makes a real difference: pat the salmon dry before you glaze it. A wet fillet steams instead of caramelizing, and you'll lose that sticky, lacquered finish.

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 large head broccoli, cut into florets
- 2 tablespoons olive oil (for broccoli)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)
- Cooked white rice for serving

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with foil.
2. Whisk together honey, minced garlic, soy sauce, 1 tablespoon olive oil, and sesame oil in a small bowl.
3. Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Place on one half of the sheet pan. Spoon glaze generously over each fillet.
4. Toss broccoli florets with 2 tablespoons olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on the other half of the pan.
5. Roast 18-20 minutes until salmon flakes easily with a fork and broccoli edges are lightly charred.
6. Serve over white rice. Sprinkle with sesame seeds if using.

## Tuesday: Ground Turkey Taco Skillet

After a Monday at work and whatever Tuesday brings — soccer practice, homework meltdowns, the general chaos of 5:30pm — this is the dinner that saves you. One skillet, 20 minutes, and your 8-year-old will ask for seconds.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 1 packet taco seasoning
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 can (15 oz) corn, drained
- 1 can (10 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles
- 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- Flour tortillas or tortilla chips for serving
- Toppings: sour cream, sliced avocado, fresh cilantro

### Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add ground turkey. Cook, breaking it apart, until no pink remains — about 6-7 minutes.
3. Stir in taco seasoning, black beans, corn, and diced tomatoes with chiles. Reduce heat to medium and simmer 5 minutes.
4. Sprinkle shredded cheese over the top. Cover the skillet for 2 minutes until cheese melts.
5. Serve straight from the skillet with tortillas or chips. Top with sour cream, avocado, and cilantro.

## Wednesday: Slow Cooker Chicken Tortilla Soup

Wednesday is the busiest night of the week in most households, so Wednesday's dinner starts at 7am. Dump everything into the slow cooker before you leave, and by the time you're home and everyone's changed out of their school clothes, dinner is ready. Shred the chicken, ladle into bowls, pile on the toppings.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes (hands-on) | **Cook time:** 6 hours on LOW | **Serves:** 4

Don't skip the lime at the end. A squeeze over each bowl brightens the whole thing and takes it from "good soup" to "can we have this again next week."

### Ingredients
- 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 can (15 oz) corn, drained
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
- 1 can (10 oz) red enchilada sauce
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Toppings: tortilla strips, shredded cheddar, sour cream, lime wedges

### Instructions
1. Place chicken breasts in the bottom of the slow cooker.
2. Add black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, enchilada sauce, and chicken broth. Stir in cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
3. Cover and cook on LOW for 6 hours or HIGH for 3 hours.
4. Remove chicken breasts and shred with two forks. Return shredded chicken to the pot and stir.
5. Ladle into bowls and top with tortilla strips, shredded cheddar, sour cream, and a squeeze of lime.

## Thursday: Garlic Butter Pasta with Italian Sausage

Thursday night pasta is a tradition in a lot of households for good reason. This one comes together in 25 minutes and uses a technique worth knowing: a splash of starchy pasta water emulsifies the butter and Parmesan into a light, glossy sauce that coats every piece of rigatoni. No jarred sauce, no cream, nothing complicated.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 12 oz rigatoni or penne pasta
- 1 lb Italian sausage (mild or hot), casings removed
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 1/2 cup pasta cooking water (reserved)
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Cook pasta in well-salted boiling water according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, brown Italian sausage in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it into crumbles, about 7-8 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to the same skillet. Once melted, add garlic and cook 60 seconds until fragrant — don't let it brown.
4. Add drained pasta to the skillet. Pour in reserved pasta water a splash at a time, tossing until you have a light, glossy sauce.
5. Return sausage to the pan. Toss everything together. Remove from heat and stir in Parmesan.
6. Finish with parsley and red pepper flakes. Taste and adjust salt and pepper.

## Friday: Sheet Pan Pork Tenderloin with Sweet Potatoes and Green Beans

Friday gets the most involved recipe of the week — but "most involved" here means 15 minutes of hands-on work and 30 minutes in the oven while you pour yourself something and decompress. Pork tenderloin is one of the most underused weeknight proteins. It's lean, it cooks fast, and the Dijon-maple rub here is genuinely good. The sweet potatoes and green beans roast right alongside it.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes | **Cook time:** 30 minutes | **Serves:** 4

Pull the pork at exactly 145°F — any higher and it starts to dry out. Let it rest 5 minutes before you slice it and it'll stay juicy.

### Ingredients
- 1.5 lbs pork tenderloin
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed into 1-inch pieces
- 2 cups green beans, trimmed
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with foil and coat lightly with oil.
2. Whisk together Dijon mustard, maple syrup, smoked paprika, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
3. Toss sweet potato cubes with 2 tablespoons olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on the sheet pan. Roast 10 minutes while you prep the pork.
4. Pat pork tenderloin dry. Rub all over with the Dijon-maple mixture. Nestle onto the sheet pan alongside the sweet potatoes.
5. Add green beans to the pan, toss with remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper.
6. Roast 25-30 minutes until pork reaches an internal temperature of 145°F and sweet potatoes are fork-tender.
7. Let pork rest 5 minutes before slicing. Serve with vegetables.
