# Hidden Veggie Mac and Cheese Kids Actually Love

This is the mac and cheese that finally solved the vegetable standoff at our dinner table. The secret is blending butternut squash, carrots, and cauliflower into the cheese sauce until they completely disappear — your kids get a full serving of vegetables without knowing it.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Vegetables are completely invisible.** The puree blends seamlessly into the cheese sauce with no detectable texture or taste.
- **Extra creamy and rich.** The vegetable puree actually makes the sauce silkier than regular mac and cheese.
- **Kid taste-tested.** This has fooled even the pickiest eaters who claim they "hate vegetables."
- **Flexible serving options.** Serve straight from the stovetop or bake it casserole-style with a crispy top.
- **Sneaky nutrition boost.** Each serving packs vitamin A, fiber, and antioxidants without changing the comfort food experience.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb elbow macaroni or shells
- 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed (about 3 cups)
- 1 large carrot, peeled and chopped
- 1/2 cup cauliflower florets
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2.5 cups whole milk
- 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
- 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- Optional: 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs for topping

## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add butternut squash, carrot, and cauliflower. Cook 12-15 minutes until very tender.
2. Remove vegetables with a slotted spoon and transfer to a blender. Reserve the cooking water.
3. Add pasta to the same boiling water and cook according to package directions until al dente. Drain.
4. While pasta cooks, blend the cooked vegetables with 1/2 cup of the reserved cooking water until completely smooth.
5. In the same large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook 1 minute.
6. Gradually whisk in milk and cook 3-4 minutes until thickened.
7. Remove from heat and stir in the vegetable puree, cheddar cheese, cream cheese, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until smooth.
8. Add cooked pasta and toss until well coated. Serve immediately, or transfer to a baking dish, top with breadcrumbs, and bake at 375°F for 15 minutes until golden.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Different vegetables:** Sweet potato works just as well as butternut squash. Yellow bell peppers and yellow summer squash also blend invisibly into the orange cheese sauce.

**Make it ahead:** The vegetable puree can be made up to 3 days in advance and stored in the fridge. Just reheat gently before adding to the cheese sauce.

**Protein boost:** Stir in diced rotisserie chicken, cooked ground turkey, or white beans for a complete meal.

**Dairy-free version:** Use plant milk and dairy-free cheese alternatives. The vegetable puree helps maintain the creamy texture.

**Freeze for later:** This freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat with a splash of milk to restore creaminess.

**Extra cheese flavor:** Mix in 1/4 cup parmesan cheese along with the cheddar for more depth without changing the kid-friendly taste.
