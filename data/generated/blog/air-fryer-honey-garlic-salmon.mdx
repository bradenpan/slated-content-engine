# Air Fryer Honey Garlic Salmon — 20-Minute High Protein Dinner

This salmon hits the table in 20 minutes and delivers the kind of protein your family needs on a busy weeknight. The honey garlic glaze caramelizes perfectly in the air fryer, giving you restaurant-quality results without any fuss.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 20 minutes** from start to finish, including preheating the air fryer
- **High protein powerhouse** with 35+ grams of protein per serving
- **No oil splattering** or fishy kitchen smells — the air fryer contains everything
- **Kid-friendly flavor** with sweet honey balancing the savory garlic
- **Minimal cleanup** with just one bowl for the sauce and the air fryer basket

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, about 6 oz each, skin removed
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- 2 green onions, sliced thin
- Optional: lemon wedges for serving

## Instructions

1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F for 3 minutes.
2. Pat salmon fillets dry and season with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, olive oil, and grated ginger.
4. Brush half the honey garlic mixture over the top of each salmon fillet.
5. Place salmon in air fryer basket, leaving space between fillets. Cook 10-12 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork.
6. Brush remaining honey garlic sauce over cooked salmon and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
7. Let rest 2 minutes, then garnish with green onions and serve with lemon wedges.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Different fish:** This glaze works beautifully with cod, mahi-mahi, or halibut. Adjust cooking time based on thickness — thinner fillets need 8-10 minutes.

**Make it spicier:** Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a teaspoon of sriracha to the honey garlic mixture.

**No fresh ginger:** Use 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger instead, or skip it entirely — the garlic and honey carry the flavor.

**Frozen salmon:** Thaw completely first and pat extra dry. Frozen fillets will steam instead of getting that nice caramelized surface.

**Prep ahead:** Mix the honey garlic sauce up to 2 days in advance. The flavors actually improve as they sit together.

**Side dish ideas:** Serve over rice, with roasted vegetables, or alongside a simple green salad for a complete high-protein meal.
