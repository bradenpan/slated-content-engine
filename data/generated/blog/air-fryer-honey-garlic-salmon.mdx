# Air Fryer Honey Garlic Salmon — 20-Minute High Protein Dinner

This salmon hits the dinner table in 20 minutes flat and delivers 35 grams of protein per serving. The honey garlic glaze gives it just enough sweetness to keep everyone happy, including kids who usually skip the fish.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 20 minutes.** Five minutes prep, 12 minutes cooking, done.
- **Set-it-and-forget-it.** Air fryer does all the work while you handle sides.
- **High protein powerhouse.** Each serving packs 35+ grams of protein to keep everyone full.
- **Kid-approved flavor.** Sweet honey balances the garlic, making this fish they'll actually eat.
- **No fishy smell.** Air fryer keeps cooking odors contained, unlike pan-searing.
- **Minimal cleanup.** One bowl for the glaze, air fryer basket goes in the dishwasher.

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, 6 oz each, skin removed
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- 2 green onions, sliced thin

## Instructions

1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F for 3 minutes.
2. Pat salmon fillets dry and season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, olive oil, and grated ginger.
4. Brush half the honey garlic mixture over the top of each salmon fillet.
5. Place salmon in air fryer basket, leaving space between fillets. Cook 10-12 minutes until fish flakes easily with a fork.
6. Remove salmon and brush with remaining honey garlic glaze.
7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and sliced green onions before serving.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Different fish:** This glaze works perfectly with cod, mahi-mahi, or halibut. Adjust cooking time based on thickness.

**Make it spicier:** Add 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes to the glaze, or drizzle with sriracha before serving.

**Swap the sweetener:** Use maple syrup instead of honey for a deeper flavor, or brown sugar if that's what you have.

**Prep ahead:** Mix the glaze up to 2 days in advance. Pat salmon dry and season right before cooking for best results.

**Serve with:** Steamed broccoli and rice make this a complete meal. The glaze is also great drizzled over the vegetables.

**Frozen salmon:** Thaw completely first, then pat extra dry. Cooking time stays the same.
