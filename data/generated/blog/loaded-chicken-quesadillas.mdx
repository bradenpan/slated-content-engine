# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Easy Dinners Everyone Will Eat

Your whole week of dinners, solved. Five crowd-pleasing meals that work for picky eaters and adventurous ones alike. Here's what's on the menu this week.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Honey Garlic Sheet Pan Chicken | 35 min |
| Tuesday | Cheesy Beef and Rice Skillet | 25 min |
| Wednesday | Baked Ziti | 45 min |
| Thursday | Teriyaki Salmon with Rice | 20 min |
| Friday | Loaded Chicken Quesadillas | 25 min |

---

## Monday: Honey Garlic Sheet Pan Chicken

Start the week with a sweet and savory winner. The honey garlic glaze makes even the pickiest eaters ask for seconds, and everything cooks on one pan.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
- 3 cups baby potatoes, halved
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together honey, minced garlic, soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon olive oil in a small bowl.
3. Season chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and paprika. Place on one side of the sheet pan.
4. Toss potatoes and broccoli with remaining olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange around chicken.
5. Brush chicken with half the honey garlic sauce. Roast for 20 minutes.
6. Brush chicken with remaining sauce and roast 5 more minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.

## Tuesday: Cheesy Beef and Rice Skillet

Tuesday calls for comfort food that comes together in one pan. This cheesy, hearty skillet meal delivers exactly that with minimal cleanup.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 cup long-grain white rice
- 2 cups beef broth
- 1 can diced tomatoes, drained
- 1 cup frozen corn
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Green onions for garnish

### Instructions
1. Brown ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks, about 6 minutes.
2. Drain excess fat. Add rice, beef broth, diced tomatoes, onion powder, and garlic powder.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 15 minutes until rice is tender.
4. Stir in frozen corn and cook 2 minutes until heated through.
5. Remove from heat and sprinkle with cheese. Cover for 2 minutes to melt cheese.
6. Garnish with sliced green onions before serving.

## Wednesday: Baked Ziti

Mid-week comfort food that feels like a warm hug. This baked ziti combines Italian sausage, creamy ricotta, and melted cheese in a way that makes everyone happy.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes | **Cook time:** 30 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 12 oz ziti pasta
- 1 lb Italian sausage, casings removed
- 3 cups marinara sauce
- 1 cup ricotta cheese
- 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 egg
- 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Cook ziti according to package directions until just shy of al dente. Drain.
2. Brown Italian sausage in a large skillet, breaking it up as it cooks, about 8 minutes. Drain fat.
3. Stir marinara sauce into the sausage and simmer 5 minutes.
4. In a bowl, mix ricotta, 1 cup mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, basil, and Italian seasoning.
5. In a greased 9x13 baking dish, layer half the pasta, all the ricotta mixture, remaining pasta, then sauce mixture.
6. Top with remaining mozzarella. Cover with foil and bake 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake 5 more minutes until bubbly.

## Thursday: Teriyaki Salmon with Rice

Thursday night gets a healthy upgrade with this quick teriyaki salmon. The sweet and savory glaze makes fish feel approachable for the whole family.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 3 tablespoons brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups cooked white rice
- 1 cup steamed broccoli
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Sesame seeds for garnish

### Instructions
1. Whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, cornstarch, ginger, and garlic in a small bowl.
2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Season salmon with salt and pepper. Cook skin-side up for 4 minutes until golden.
4. Flip salmon and cook 3 more minutes.
5. Pour teriyaki sauce over salmon and cook 2 minutes until sauce thickens and salmon flakes easily.
6. Serve over rice with steamed broccoli. Garnish with sesame seeds.

## Friday: Loaded Chicken Quesadillas

End the week with everyone's favorite: quesadillas loaded with chicken, cheese, and vegetables. Cut into wedges and watch them disappear.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
- 8 large flour tortillas
- 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1/2 red onion, diced
- 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Sour cream and salsa for serving

### Instructions
1. Mix shredded chicken with cumin and chili powder in a bowl.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté bell pepper and onion until soft, about 5 minutes.
3. On 4 tortillas, layer cheese, seasoned chicken, sautéed vegetables, and black beans. Top with remaining tortillas.
4. Heat remaining oil in the skillet. Cook quesadillas one at a time, 2-3 minutes per side until golden and cheese melts.
5. Cut into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa.
