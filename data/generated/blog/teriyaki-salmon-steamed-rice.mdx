# This Week's Family Dinner Plan — 5 Easy Dinners Everyone Will Eat

Your whole week of dinners, planned. Five meals that please everyone at the table, from the pickiest eater to the adventurous one. Here's what your family is eating this week.

## This Week's Plan

| Day | Meal | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Sheet Pan Honey Garlic Chicken | 35 min |
| Tuesday | Beef and Cheese Quesadillas | 25 min |
| Wednesday | Creamy Chicken and Rice Skillet | 25 min |
| Thursday | Baked Ziti with Hidden Vegetables | 45 min |
| Friday | Teriyaki Salmon with Steamed Rice | 20 min |

---

## Monday: Sheet Pan Honey Garlic Chicken

Start your week with this sweet and savory crowd-pleaser. Everything cooks together on one pan while you help with homework or catch up on the day.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together honey, garlic, soy sauce, 1 tablespoon olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
3. Place chicken on one side of the pan. Toss potatoes and broccoli with remaining olive oil, salt, and pepper on the other side.
4. Brush chicken with half the honey garlic mixture.
5. Bake 20 minutes, then brush chicken with remaining sauce. Bake 5 more minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.

---

## Tuesday: Beef and Cheese Quesadillas

Tuesday calls for something simple and satisfying. These quesadillas disappear as fast as you can make them, and everyone can customize their toppings.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 packet taco seasoning
- 8 flour tortillas
- 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1 small onion, diced
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Sour cream and salsa for serving

### Instructions
1. Cook ground beef, onion, and bell pepper in a large skillet over medium-high heat until beef is browned, about 8 minutes.
2. Drain fat, add taco seasoning and water according to package directions. Simmer 3 minutes.
3. Place filling and cheese on half of each tortilla, then fold over.
4. Heat oil in a clean skillet over medium heat. Cook quesadillas 2-3 minutes per side until golden and cheese melts.
5. Cut into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa.

## Wednesday: Creamy Chicken and Rice Skillet

Mid-week comfort food that comes together in one pan. The rice absorbs all the flavors while the vegetables add color and nutrition without any complaints.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 cup long-grain white rice
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.
2. Cook chicken until golden, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Add onion to the same skillet and cook 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.
4. Add rice and stir 1 minute. Pour in chicken broth and bring to a boil.
5. Return chicken to skillet, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer 15 minutes.
6. Stir in frozen vegetables and cream. Cook 2 more minutes until heated through.

## Thursday: Baked Ziti with Hidden Vegetables

Everyone loves pasta night, and this version sneaks in extra nutrition without anyone noticing. The vegetables melt into the sauce, adding sweetness and texture.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes | **Cook time:** 30 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 1 lb ziti pasta
- 1 lb ground turkey
- 2 cups marinara sauce
- 1 cup ricotta cheese
- 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
- 1 zucchini, finely diced
- 1 carrot, finely diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Cook pasta according to package directions, drain.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet. Cook onion, carrot, and zucchini until softened, about 5 minutes.
3. Add garlic and ground turkey. Cook until turkey is browned, about 8 minutes.
4. Stir in marinara sauce and Italian seasoning. Simmer 5 minutes.
5. In a large bowl, combine pasta, meat mixture, ricotta, and 1 cup mozzarella.
6. Transfer to a greased 9x13 baking dish. Top with remaining mozzarella.
7. Bake 25-30 minutes until bubbly and cheese is golden.

## Friday: Teriyaki Salmon with Steamed Rice

End the week with something a little special but still weeknight-friendly. The sweet teriyaki glaze makes salmon appealing even to hesitant fish eaters.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Serves:** 4

### Ingredients
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 2 cups jasmine rice, cooked
- 1 bag (12 oz) frozen stir-fry vegetables
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together teriyaki sauce, honey, and sesame oil.
3. Season salmon with salt and pepper, place on baking sheet.
4. Brush salmon with half the teriyaki mixture. Bake 12-15 minutes until fish flakes easily.
5. Meanwhile, cook frozen vegetables according to package directions.
6. Serve salmon over rice with vegetables. Drizzle with remaining sauce and top with green onions and sesame seeds.
