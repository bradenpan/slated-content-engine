# One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetables

This is the kind of dinner that makes you feel like you have your act together on a Tuesday night. Everything cooks on one pan, the chicken stays juicy, and the spring vegetables actually taste like spring.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True 30-minute meal** from prep to table, including oven time
- **One pan, minimal cleanup** - just line with parchment and toss when done
- **Perfectly timed cooking** - potatoes get a head start, then everything finishes together
- **Fresh spring flavors** that feel light but satisfying
- **Kid-friendly vegetables** - even picky eaters usually go for roasted asparagus and snap peas
- **Scales easily** for meal prep or feeding a crowd

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken thighs, about 1.5 lbs
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
- 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 cup sugar snap peas
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Pat chicken thighs dry and season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, thyme, and rosemary.
4. Place chicken thighs on one side of the prepared pan. Brush with half the lemon herb mixture.
5. Toss halved potatoes with remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil and place on the other side of the pan.
6. Roast for 15 minutes.
7. Add asparagus and snap peas to the pan, drizzle with remaining lemon herb mixture, and toss gently.
8. Continue roasting 8-10 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and vegetables are tender.
9. Let rest 5 minutes, then garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Chicken breasts work too - just pound them to even thickness and reduce cooking time by 3-4 minutes. Salmon fillets are delicious with these same flavors.

**Different vegetables:** Broccoli, Brussels sprouts, or green beans all work well. Zucchini and bell peppers need less time - add them with the asparagus.

**Make it dairy-free:** This recipe is naturally dairy-free as written. For extra richness, finish with a pat of butter on the hot chicken.

**Herb alternatives:** No fresh herbs? Use 1 teaspoon each dried thyme and rosemary. Dried herbs go in the marinade, not as garnish.

**Meal prep friendly:** Leftovers reheat beautifully. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days or freeze individual portions for up to 3 months.

**Make ahead:** Marinate the chicken in the lemon herb mixture up to 24 hours ahead. The vegetables can be prepped and stored separately.
