# One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetables

This is the kind of dinner that makes a Tuesday feel manageable. Everything cooks together on one sheet pan, the chicken stays juicy, and the vegetables actually taste like something your family will eat. Plus, cleanup is just one pan and a cutting board.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True 30-minute timing** from start to table, including prep time.
- **One sheet pan** means minimal cleanup on a busy weeknight.
- **Spring vegetables cook perfectly** alongside the chicken without getting mushy.
- **Kid-friendly flavors** with bright lemon and herbs that aren't too intense.
- **Scales easily** for meal prep or feeding a crowd this weekend.
- **Uses pantry staples** you probably already have on hand.

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, about 6 oz each
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
- 1 lb asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 8 oz sugar snap peas
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together lemon juice, lemon zest, olive oil, garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
3. Add chicken breasts to the bowl and toss to coat. Let marinate while you prep the vegetables.
4. Add halved potatoes to the same bowl with remaining marinade and toss well.
5. Arrange chicken and potatoes on the prepared sheet pan. Roast for 12 minutes.
6. Add asparagus and snap peas to the pan, tossing with any remaining juices.
7. Continue roasting 8-10 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and vegetables are tender.
8. Let rest 5 minutes, then garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Chicken thighs work beautifully here and stay even more tender. Pork tenderloin or salmon fillets are great alternatives too.

**Different vegetables:** Use whatever spring vegetables look good. Broccoli, green beans, zucchini, or Brussels sprouts all roast well in the same timeframe.

**Make it dairy-free:** This recipe is naturally dairy-free as written, perfect for anyone avoiding dairy.

**Prep ahead:** Marinate the chicken and prep all vegetables the night before. Store everything separately in the fridge, then just assemble and roast.

**Leftover magic:** Shred any leftover chicken and toss with the roasted vegetables over salad greens for tomorrow's lunch.

**Herb substitutions:** Dried herbs work in a pinch. Use 1 teaspoon dried thyme and 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary instead of fresh.
