# One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken with Spring Vegetables

This is the kind of dinner that makes a Tuesday night feel manageable. Everything goes on one pan, the oven does the work, and cleanup takes about two minutes. The lemon herb seasoning brightens up the whole dish and makes even the pickiest eaters ask for seconds.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **True one-pan cooking.** Sheet pan, parchment paper, done. Minimal dishes to wash.
- **30 minutes total,** including a 10-minute head start for the potatoes.
- **Spring vegetables at their best.** Asparagus, cherry tomatoes, and zucchini cook perfectly alongside the chicken.
- **Kid-friendly flavors.** Lemon and herbs are familiar, not overwhelming.
- **Scales easily.** Double it for meal prep or feeding a crowd.
- **Flexible timing.** Potatoes get a head start, everything else finishes together.

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, about 6 oz each
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
- 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 pint cherry tomatoes
- 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 lemons, juiced and zested
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Optional: fresh parsley for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
2. Season chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, oregano, and thyme.
4. Add halved potatoes to the bowl and toss to coat. Arrange on one side of the prepared sheet pan.
5. Bake potatoes for 10 minutes while you prep the remaining ingredients.
6. Add chicken breasts to the same bowl with remaining herb mixture and toss to coat.
7. Remove pan from oven. Add chicken to the center, then scatter asparagus, cherry tomatoes, and zucchini around the pan.
8. Drizzle any remaining herb mixture over the vegetables.
9. Bake 15-20 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and vegetables are tender.
10. Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Swap the protein:** Chicken thighs work beautifully here - just increase cooking time to 25-30 minutes. Salmon fillets need only 12-15 minutes total.

**Different vegetables:** Broccoli, bell peppers, or green beans all work. Root vegetables like carrots need the same head start as potatoes.

**Make it heartier:** Serve over rice, quinoa, or pasta. The pan juices make an excellent sauce.

**Prep ahead:** Cut vegetables and mix the herb oil the night before. Store separately in the fridge and assemble when ready to cook.

**Dairy-free option:** This recipe is naturally dairy-free as written. Add a sprinkle of parmesan at the end if your family tolerates dairy.

**Storage tips:** Leftovers keep for 3 days in the fridge and reheat well in the microwave or a 350°F oven.
