# Air Fryer Honey Garlic Salmon — High Protein Dinner Ready in 20 Minutes

This salmon hits that sweet spot between impressive enough for company and easy enough for a Tuesday. The honey garlic glaze caramelizes perfectly in the air fryer, and you get flaky, restaurant-quality fish without any of the fuss.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Actually 20 minutes** from start to finish, including preheating time
- **No flipping required** — the air fryer does all the work
- **Kid-friendly sweet glaze** that makes fish approachable for picky eaters  
- **High protein powerhouse** with 35+ grams per serving
- **Minimal cleanup** — just the air fryer basket and a small mixing bowl
- **Scales easily** for meal prep or feeding a crowd

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, about 6 oz each
- 3 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- 2 green onions, sliced thin

## Instructions

1. Preheat air fryer to 400°F for 3 minutes.
2. Pat salmon fillets dry and season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, minced garlic, olive oil, and ginger.
4. Brush half the honey garlic mixture over the top of each salmon fillet.
5. Place salmon in air fryer basket, leaving space between fillets. Cook 7 minutes.
6. Brush remaining glaze over salmon and cook 3-5 minutes more, until fish flakes easily with a fork.
7. Let rest 2 minutes, then sprinkle with sesame seeds and green onions before serving.

## Tips and Substitutions

**Different fish:** This glaze works beautifully with cod, mahi-mahi, or chicken thighs (increase cooking time to 18-20 minutes for chicken).

**Make it spicier:** Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a teaspoon of sriracha to the glaze mixture.

**No fresh ginger:** Use 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger instead, or skip it entirely — the garlic and honey carry the flavor.

**Meal prep friendly:** Cook extra fillets and flake the leftovers into grain bowls or salads throughout the week.

**Serve with:** Steamed broccoli, roasted asparagus, or a simple side salad. Rice or quinoa makes it more filling for hungry teenagers.

**Glaze variations:** Swap the soy sauce for teriyaki sauce, or use maple syrup instead of honey for a different flavor profile.
