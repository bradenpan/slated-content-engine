# How to Get Your Family to Agree on Dinner Before You Start Cooking

You planned it. You shopped for it. You cooked it. And then someone says,
"I don't want that." Sound familiar?

The problem is rarely the food. It is that nobody had a say in the plan.
Here are five ways to fix that.

## 1. Give Choices, Not Open Questions

Asking "what do you want for dinner?" invites chaos. Instead, offer two or
three options and let people pick. Constraints make decisions easier.

...

## 2. Let Everyone Weigh In Once a Week

Instead of nightly negotiations, batch the input. One conversation a week
(or one vote) about the plan for the next five nights.

...

## 3. Build Around Non-Negotiables

Every family has them -- the one dish everyone likes, the ingredient someone
cannot eat. Start there, and build the rest of the week around the anchors.

...

## 4. Make the Plan Visible

A plan that lives in your head carries no weight. When the plan is written
down and shared, complaints drop because expectations are set.

...

## 5. Accept the 80% Rule

Not every meal will be everyone's favorite. The goal is not perfection.
It is that most nights, most people are happy. That is a win.
