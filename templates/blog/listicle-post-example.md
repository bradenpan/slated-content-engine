# 7 Easy Weeknight Dinners Your Family Will Actually Eat

Not seven *aspirational* dinners. Seven dinners that actually happen on a
Tuesday when you are tired and the kids are hungry. All under 30 minutes.
All family-tested.

## 1. Sheet Pan Chicken Fajitas

Slice everything, throw it on a pan, set a timer. Peppers, onions, chicken,
and a packet of fajita seasoning. Serve with tortillas and whatever toppings
are already in the fridge.

**Time:** 25 minutes | **Protein:** Chicken

---

## 2. One-Pot Pasta with Italian Sausage

Everything goes in one pot -- pasta, sausage, canned tomatoes, spinach.
No draining, no separate sauce. Dinner and cleanup in the same pan.

**Time:** 20 minutes | **Protein:** Italian sausage

---

## 3. Teriyaki Salmon with Rice

Pan-sear the salmon, pour bottled teriyaki sauce over it, serve with rice
and steamed broccoli. Tastes like takeout, costs a fraction.

**Time:** 15 minutes | **Protein:** Salmon

---

> **These are just seven dinners. Want the other four nights handled too?**
> Slated builds a personalized weekly plan around your family's preferences
> -- and everyone gets to vote on it before you buy the groceries.

---

## 4. BBQ Chicken Quesadillas

Shredded rotisserie chicken + BBQ sauce + cheese in a tortilla.
Three ingredients, ten minutes.

**Time:** 10 minutes | **Protein:** Chicken (rotisserie)

---

## 5. Beef and Broccoli Stir Fry

Thin-sliced beef, broccoli, soy sauce, garlic. Over rice in 20 minutes.
Better than takeout, and you control the sodium.

**Time:** 20 minutes | **Protein:** Beef

---

## 6. Baked Pesto Chicken with Roasted Tomatoes

Chicken breasts, pesto, cherry tomatoes. 375F for 20 minutes.
Serve with crusty bread or pasta.

**Time:** 25 minutes | **Protein:** Chicken

---

## 7. 15-Minute Shrimp Tacos

Sauteed shrimp, lime, cabbage slaw, flour tortillas.
The fastest taco night you will ever have.

**Time:** 15 minutes | **Protein:** Shrimp

---

Seven dinners. One grocery trip. Zero arguments. That is a good week.

**Key structural notes for the AI generator:**
- Number in the title (drives saves on Pinterest)
- Each entry is substantive (2-3 sentences, not a one-liner)
- Quick-reference info per entry (time, protein)
- Mid-post CTA after entry 3 (natural transition)
- End-of-post BlogCTA component
- Conversational wrap-up
- Total length: 800-1,200 words
