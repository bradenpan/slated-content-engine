# **Slated Brand Guidelines**

Version: 2.2 (Persona-Aesthetic Alignment)  
Date: December 2025

## **1\. Brand Identity**

### **The Name**

Slated.  
(Pronounced: /sleÉªtÉªd/)

* **Definition:** Scheduled. Planned. Set in stone.  
* **The Pivot:** We take the hard efficiency of "Slated" and wrap it in the warmth of hospitality.  
* **The Archetype:** **The Kitchen Chief of Staff.**  
  * We are not a "Chatbot" (too tech).  
  * We are not a "Sous Chef" (too subservient).  
  * We are a **Chief of Staff**: We anticipate needs, present finished plans for the family's review, and handle the details so the parent doesn't have to.

### **The Feeling: "Cognitive Relief"**

The core emotion we sell is The 5 o'clock Exhale.  
It is the specific feeling of relief a parent gets when they realize the thinking is already done, the decision is made, and the groceries are already there.

### **Taglines & Positioning**

* **Consumer Tagline:** "Dinner, Done."  
* **Secondary:** "The plan is ready. You just have to say yes."  
* **Internal Positioning:** "The Kitchen Chief of Staff."

## **2\. Brand Pillars**

These are the three non-negotiable promises we make to the "Chief Household Officer."

### **1\. Anticipatory Convenience (The Proposal)**

* **The Old Way:** "Search for what you want." (High Cognitive Load).  
* **The Slated Way:** "Here is a plan we made for you." (Zero Cognitive Load).  
* **The Promise:** We do not wait to be asked. Like a good Chief of Staff, we present a **Proposal** (The Draft) based on inventory and history. The userâ€™s only job is to **Agree**, not to **Invent**.

### **2\. Reality-Proof Flexibility (The Red Pen)**

* **The Old Way:** "Follow this recipe exactly." (Rigid).  
* **The Slated Way:** "Edit this plan to fit your reality." (Fluid).  
* **The Promise:** Plans survive contact with reality. When the user says "I only have 20 minutes" or "Make this dairy-free," we rewrite the plan instantly. We don't force a grocery run; we adapt the logic.

### **3\. The Peacekeeper (The Shield)**

* **The Old Way:** The parent fights with the family about dinner.  
* **The Slated Way:** The family fights with the App.  
* **The Promise:** We democratize the friction. By creating a **Shared View** (The Table) where the family votes, we remove the parent from the line of fire. We are the "Neutral Party" that validates the menu.

## **3\. The Slated Lexicon (Critical)**

We use specific words to reinforce our position as a "System," not just a recipe book, while maintaining the warmth of a home.

| Don't Say... | Do Say... | Why? |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Search** | **The Proposal** | "Search" implies work. "Proposal" implies we started it for you. |
| **Chat** | **Adjust** | We are not having a conversation; we are editing a plan. |
| **Logistics** | **The Groceries** | "Logistics" is a business unit. "Groceries" is a household task we handle. |

| **Ratify** | **Green Light** | "Ratify" is legal. "Green Light" is how families say "Go." |

## **4\. Visual Identity**

### **The Logo System: "Warm Efficiency"**

We balance the hard efficiency of the name "Slated" with soft, warm visuals.

#### **A. The Icon (The Cloche)**

* **Symbol:** The classic **Amber Cloche** with three steam lines.  
* **Meaning:** Service. Hospitality. "We cooked this for you."  
* **Usage:** App Icon, Favicon, Loading States.

#### **B. The Wordmark (Clean)**

* **Font:** **Manrope** (ExtraBold) or **Plus Jakarta Sans** (Bold).  
* **Why:** We use a modern, geometric sans-serif. It mirrors the clean aesthetic of our target persona's other tools (Apple, Tesla, Oura) while avoiding the "tech-bro" coldness of pure Helvetica. It is competent, not "cute."  
* **Color:** Stone 900 (Warm Dark Grey).

## **6\. Typography**

### **Headlines (The Brand Voice)**

**Family:** *Manrope* (Google Font)

* **Style:** Modern, Geometric, Grotesque.  
* **Usage:** App Header, Section Titles, Modal Headings.  
* **Why:** To signal "Competence." It is legible and clean, aligning with the "High-Velocity" parent's expectation of premium software.

### **Body Copy (The Utility)**

**Family:** *Inter* (Google Font)

* **Style:** Clean, standard, highly legible.  
* **Usage:** Recipes, Grocery Lists, Settings.  
* **Rule:** Do not use the rounded font for ingredient lists; it reduces readability at small sizes.

## **8\. UI & Layout Principles**

1. **Clarity Over Beauty:** If it takes an extra click to make it look pretty, don't do it. The parent is in a rush.  
2. **Big Touch Targets:** Designed for one-handed use (holding a baby/groceries).  
3. **"Glanceability":** Can I read this step from 3 feet away while the pasta boils?  
4. **The "Red Pen" State:** Text should feel mutable. Users should feel like they can tap and change anything (ingredients, serving sizes) without breaking the UI.  
5. **Warm Stone Background:** The app must always feel like it sits on a warm stone countertop (\#FAFAF9).  
6. **Soft Shadows:** Cards should have soft, diffuse shadows (shadowOpacity: 0.05), not harsh drop shadows.  
7. **No "Tech Blue":** Avoid standard hyperlink blue. Links and actions should be Emerald or Amber.
