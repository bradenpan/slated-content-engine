# TikTok Community Research: Parenting Life-Stages

**Research Date:** 2026-02-28
**Prepared for:** Slated (Smart Dinner Planning App, $7.99/month)
**Target Personas:** HVP (High-Velocity Parent), Peacekeeper
**Exclusion Personas:** Budget Optimizer, Perfectionist

---

## Research Methodology

Data gathered via web searches of TikTok hashtag pages, influencer databases (Feedspot, Favikon, Modash, Heepsy, Social Cat, HypeAuditor), news articles, and social media analytics platforms. Where exact hashtag view counts were available from TikTok tag pages or analytics tools, they are cited. Where data was unavailable or approximate, this is noted explicitly. All follower counts are approximate and reflect the most recent data available at time of research.

---

## 1. #SingleMomTok / #SingleParentTok

### Key Hashtags
- **#singlemom** -- 31.1B views, 11.4M posts (primary hashtag)
- **#singlemomtok** -- niche community tag (exact views not publicly indexed; subset of #singlemom)
- **#singleparenttok** -- niche community tag
- **#singlemomlife**, **#singleparent**, **#solomom**

### Size & Scale
This is one of the largest parenting life-stage communities on TikTok. The umbrella hashtag #singlemom alone commands 31.1 billion views and over 11.4 million posts, making it a mature, high-volume community. The "-tok" suffixed variants (#singlemomtok, #singleparenttok) function as community-identity tags used by creators who self-identify within the subculture rather than just tagging topical content.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Predominantly women aged 25-40
- Skews US-based but has international presence (UK "single mum" variant is active)
- Mix of never-married, divorced, and widowed parents
- Income spans wide range; significant representation from working-class and middle-income brackets
- High representation of BIPOC creators

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- "Day in my life" routines showing solo juggling of work + kids
- Emotional/raw content about exhaustion, loneliness, and resilience
- Financial survival content (budgeting, side hustles, food stamps)
- Comedy and self-deprecating humor about doing it all alone
- Transition/glow-up videos (before/after divorce transformations)

**Dominant themes:** Resilience, "I do it all myself," survival mode, independence, proving doubters wrong, building a life from nothing.

**Warning for Slated:** Budget-focused content is heavily represented here (food stamp budgeting, extreme couponing, dollar store meals). This means the Budget Optimizer persona is active in this community. Content strategy must carefully differentiate from budget optimization messaging.

### Key Creators
| Creator | Handle | Followers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Single Mom Saga | @singlemomsaga | ~2.1M | Comedy/lifestyle, 37M+ likes total |
| Maia Knight | @maiaknight | ~7.6M | Most famous single mom creator; now married, shifted away from kids content |
| Adrea Garza | @adreagarza | ~500K+ | Single mom of twins; went viral for Halloween costumes |

### Meal Planning Relevance: HIGH
- "What's for dinner" is a frequent pain point expressed in this community
- Creators share "easy dinners for single moms" content that gets strong engagement
- Freezer meal prep and batch cooking are popular subgenres
- The phrase "thinking through dinner EVERY DAY FOREVER is just too much to ask" resonated as a viral sentiment in this space
- Meal planning content specifically coded to single-parent life exists but is underserved by dedicated tools/apps

### Competitive Presence
- **No dedicated meal planning apps** observed advertising specifically to single mom creators
- General meal kit services (HelloFresh, EveryPlate) appear in broader mom content but not targeted to this sub-community
- Budget-focused meal planning content dominates (Aldi hauls, Walmart grocery runs) -- most competitors here are grocery stores, not apps
- The space is largely **unbranded** from a meal-planning-app perspective

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** HVP -- single parents managing everything alone epitomize "I don't want to build a plan. I want to sign off on one."
- **Key Feature:** Auto-generated weekly meal plans with dietary restriction handling. Single parents often manage kids with allergies/preferences alone without a partner to consult.
- **Angle:** "One less decision. Every night." -- Position Slated as the elimination of the daily dinner question, not as a budgeting tool. Avoid price-comparison messaging. Focus on cognitive load reduction.
- **Risk:** Community has strong Budget Optimizer presence. Slated's $7.99/month must be framed as time savings, not cost savings.

### Engagement Quality
- High emotional engagement (comments are supportive, community-building)
- Saves are strong on practical content (recipes, routines, hacks)
- Shares are strong on emotional/relatable content
- TikTok's algorithm weights saves at 10x likes, which favors the practical meal-planning content that would benefit Slated

## 2. #BlendedFamily / #StepParentTok

### Key Hashtags
- **#blendedfamily** -- 1.5B views (confirmed from TikTok tag page)
- **#stepmom** -- 205.3K posts
- **#stepmother** -- 18.4K posts
- **#stepparenttok**, **#stepmomtok**, **#bonusmom**, **#stepmomlife**
- **#blendedfamilylife**, **#stepparenting**

### Size & Scale
A mid-sized but highly engaged community. The #blendedfamily hashtag at 1.5 billion views indicates significant reach. The step-parent variants (#stepmom, #stepparenttok, #stepmomtok) fragment the community across multiple tags, meaning total community size is larger than any single hashtag suggests. Combined, the blended family ecosystem likely exceeds 2-3B views.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Predominantly women (stepmoms), aged 28-45
- Heavily US-based with UK/Australia secondary
- Higher proportion of married/partnered individuals (by definition)
- Often managing 3-6+ children across biological and step-relationships
- Household income varies but skews middle-income (dual-income households with child support dynamics)

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- Emotional content about the challenges of step-parenting (feeling like an outsider, "you're not my real mom")
- Wins and wholesome moments (step-child calling stepmom "mom" for the first time)
- Blended family logistics and hacks (merging households, custody schedules)
- Humor about managing different sets of rules across households
- Holiday and meal content showing large combined families

**Dominant themes:** Identity validation ("I am a real parent"), navigating complex family dynamics, finding harmony, dealing with ex-spouses, building new traditions.

### Key Creators
| Creator | Handle | Followers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Avery Woods | (various handles) | ~1.7M | Mom of 4, step-mom content, realistic routines |
| Christine Andrew | @christineandrew | ~751K | Blended family of 6 |
| AreTheKidsAllYours | @arethekidsallyours | Est. 100K+ | Large blended family parenting hacks, 60.8K likes on individual posts |

### Meal Planning Relevance: VERY HIGH
- Blended families face the **unique challenge of merging food preferences, allergies, and habits** across two (or more) previously separate households
- "Kids from different houses want different things" is a recurring theme
- Step-parents often inherit dietary restrictions they didn't plan for
- Custody schedules mean meal planning must account for variable household size (3 kids this week, 5 kids next week)
- Holiday meals and "whose tradition do we follow" content is a major subgenre

### Competitive Presence
- **No meal planning apps** observed targeting blended families specifically
- Some family organization apps (Cozi, OurHome) have adjacent presence but don't address meal planning with variable household size
- Co-parenting apps (TalkingParents, OurFamilyWizard) exist in this space but focus on scheduling/communication, not meals
- This is a **white space** for meal planning technology

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** Peacekeeper -- "Your family's input before dinner. Not their complaints after." This is the *ideal* Peacekeeper community. The emotional labor of feeding a blended family where kids have different preferences and histories is exactly what the Peacekeeper carries.
- **Key Feature:** "Dinner Draft" family voting feature is a perfect fit. It lets step-kids and bio-kids all have a voice, reducing the "you're not my real mom, I don't want your food" dynamic.
- **Secondary Feature:** Dietary restriction and allergy management across variable household composition
- **Angle:** "Everyone gets a vote. No one gets a veto." Frame Dinner Draft as a peacemaking tool that gives every family member agency.
- **Strategic note:** This is arguably Slated's highest-fit community. The combination of variable household size, merged preferences, and emotional labor of feeding makes every Slated feature relevant.

### Engagement Quality
- Very high comment engagement (emotional support, advice-sharing)
- Strong save rates on logistics/hack content
- Share rates moderate (content is personal/vulnerable, people share less publicly)
- Duets and stitches are common (creators responding to each other's step-parenting challenges)

## 3. #MilitarySpouse / #MilSpouseTok

### Key Hashtags
- **#militaryspouse** -- active tag page on TikTok (exact view count not publicly indexed in search results)
- **#milspousetok** -- 76.8M views
- **#militarywife**, **#milso**, **#miltok**
- **#deploymentlife**, **#militaryfamily**, **#pcsmove**
- **#militaryspouselife**, **#militaryspousesoftiktok**

### Size & Scale
A tight-knit but passionate niche community. The #milspousetok tag at 76.8M views represents the community-identity tag; the broader #militaryspouse umbrella is significantly larger (likely in the low billions when combined with #militarywife and related tags). The community is smaller than single-parent or blended-family communities but has notably high cohesion and loyalty.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Predominantly women aged 22-38
- Almost exclusively US-based (some NATO-ally presence)
- Many are stay-at-home parents by necessity (frequent moves make career continuity difficult)
- Young families with children under 10 are heavily represented
- Geographic distribution follows military base locations but content frequently features overseas duty stations (Germany, Japan, Korea)
- Education level varies; many have college degrees but underemployment is common

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- Deployment homecoming videos (extremely high engagement, viral potential)
- "Solo parenting during deployment" content -- raw, honest, sometimes tearful
- PCS (Permanent Change of Station) moving content -- logistics, emotional upheaval
- Military spouse humor (dependa jokes, commissary runs, "my husband's rank" satire)
- Day-in-the-life during deployment

**Dominant themes:** Resilience, loneliness, "we serve too," adaptability, making a home anywhere, community building among other mil-spouses.

### Key Creators
| Creator | Handle | Followers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Bree | @bree_mcj | ~167K | Relatable military spouse content, 7.7M likes |
| Jillian Morrison | @mrsjillianmorrison | ~135K | Marine spouse + doctoral student |
| Ali | (mil-spouse creator in Germany) | ~740K | Southern charm + cooking/recipes from overseas duty station |
| Mrs. Frazzled | @mrs.frazzled | Est. 50K+ | Military family planning and organization |

### Meal Planning Relevance: HIGH
- Solo parenting during deployment creates acute need for simplified dinner planning
- Irregular schedules (spouse home at unpredictable times) make flexible meal planning critical
- PCS moves mean constantly adapting to new grocery stores, new kitchens, new local food options
- Military spouses report using meal delivery services and digital tools (including ChatGPT) to plan meals during deployment
- One creator shared a systematic approach: "Meal Prep Monday, Tidy Tuesday, Wipe-down Wednesday" -- indicating demand for structured systems
- Commissary shopping (limited selection) creates unique constraints
- Cooking content from Ali (740K followers) demonstrates appetite for mil-spouse food content

### Competitive Presence
- **No meal planning apps** observed targeting military spouses specifically
- Meal delivery services (HelloFresh) appear but are not specifically positioned for mil-spouse life
- Some military-specific lifestyle brands (USAA, Military OneSource) are present but don't address meals
- Grocery delivery services get organic mentions as deployment survival tools
- This is **underserved territory** for food tech

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** HVP -- during deployment, military spouses are functionally single parents with zero backup. "I don't want to build a plan. I want to sign off on one" is their daily reality.
- **Key Feature:** Auto-generated weekly plans that adapt to variable household size (spouse home vs. deployed = different portion sizes). Dietary restriction handling for kids while managing alone.
- **Angle:** "Deploy-proof your dinner plan." Position Slated as the tool that stays consistent even when everything else is changing -- new base, new kitchen, spouse gone.
- **Secondary angle:** When spouse returns from deployment, Dinner Draft feature helps reintegrate them into family food decisions.
- **Strategic note:** Military spouse community is extremely brand-loyal. If Slated earns trust here (e.g., military discount, partnership with USAA or Military OneSource), word-of-mouth within the community would be powerful.

### Engagement Quality
- Extremely high engagement rates relative to follower counts
- Homecoming videos drive massive views but meal/routine content drives saves
- Strong community commenting (mil-spouses support each other actively)
- Shares within mil-spouse group chats and Facebook groups are common (cross-platform amplification)

## 4. #PostpartumTok / #FourthTrimester

### Key Hashtags
- **#newmom** -- 11.2B views (massive umbrella tag)
- **#fourthtrimester** -- 651.2M views
- **#postpartum** -- active tag (exact view count not indexed; estimated multi-billion based on content volume)
- **#postpartumtok**, **#postpartumlife**, **#newborn**
- **#firsttimemom**, **#momlife**, **#postpartumjourney**
- **#survivalmode**, **#newbornlife**

### Size & Scale
One of the largest communities researched. The #newmom tag alone at 11.2B views indicates massive scale. The #fourthtrimester tag at 651.2M views represents the more specific, identity-driven community. Combined with #postpartum and related tags, total community reach likely exceeds 15-20B views. This is a high-churn community -- parents cycle through it in 3-12 months, creating constant demand for "beginner" content.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Women aged 25-38 (first-time mothers skew 28-34)
- All income levels represented
- Geographically diverse (US, UK, Australia, Canada)
- Peak engagement during late-night/early-morning hours (feeding schedules)
- Audience is sleep-deprived, emotionally heightened, and scrolling one-handed while nursing/feeding
- Partners/fathers are a secondary audience

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- Raw, unfiltered postpartum reality (body changes, sleep deprivation, emotional struggles)
- "Things I wish I knew" content -- practical advice from slightly-more-experienced parents
- Product recommendations and "what actually helped" posts
- Humor about the chaos of newborn life
- Freezer meal prep (often done pre-birth, shared as postpartum survival strategy)
- Breastfeeding content (86M+ views on #breastfeeding)

**Dominant themes:** Survival, "it gets better," honesty about struggles, unsolicited advice frustration, identity transformation, "this is my body now."

### Key Creators
| Creator | Handle | Followers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Brie Matthews | (postpartum creator) | ~227K | Raw postpartum content, intentionally "keeping it real" |
| Brittany Noonan | (mom of 4) | Est. 200K+ | Honest postpartum content after 4th child |
| Kristy Sarah | @kristysarah | ~16.9M | Mom-life humor, pranks, viral skits |
| The Mom Trotter | @themomtrotter | ~1.8M | Travel + homeschool + mom content |
| Allison Delperdang | @allisondelperdang | Est. 100K+ | Postpartum meal prep content specifically |

### Meal Planning Relevance: VERY HIGH
- Postpartum is arguably the **peak need moment** for automated meal planning
- "Freezer meals for new moms" is an entire TikTok content channel with dedicated following
- New parents describe cooking as impossible during the fourth trimester
- Meal trains (friends/family delivering meals) are a major trend -- but they end after 2-4 weeks, leaving a gap
- Brands like Mommy's Bliss already create postpartum meal content on TikTok
- Meal delivery services (Mom's Meals) market directly to postpartum parents
- Content about "simple postpartum meal prep" regularly goes viral

### Competitive Presence
- **Mom's Meals** -- marketed as postpartum meal delivery (some covered by insurance)
- **Season Health** -- has a postpartum meal plan guide
- **Freezer meal prep content** is the dominant "solution" -- DIY, not app-driven
- **No meal planning apps** observed with specific postpartum positioning
- Meal kit companies (HelloFresh, etc.) appear in broader mom content but don't target the fourth trimester
- Baby brands (Mommy's Bliss) create adjacent meal content but aren't meal planning tools
- **Major white space** for an app that handles the post-meal-train gap

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** HVP in crisis mode -- "I can't even think about dinner. I can barely think."
- **Key Feature:** Fully automated meal plans that require zero cognitive effort. One-tap approval of a weekly plan. Integration with grocery delivery.
- **Angle:** "Your meal train ended. Slated didn't." Position as the continuation of the support system that friends and family provide temporarily.
- **Secondary Feature:** Dietary restrictions during breastfeeding (some foods affect milk supply/baby digestion) -- Slated's 16 dietary restriction handling is directly relevant.
- **Timing Strategy:** This community has a natural "acquisition window" -- reach parents during pregnancy (nesting/planning phase) for post-birth activation. Freezer meal prep content during pregnancy primes the audience for meal planning tools.
- **Risk:** Users are in survival mode and may not have bandwidth to onboard a new app. The signup flow must be frictionless.

### Engagement Quality
- Extremely high save rates (parents save everything "for later" during survival mode)
- Comments are highly supportive and advice-rich
- Late-night engagement patterns (midnight-4am is peak)
- High share rates to partners ("read this")
- TikTok's algorithm heavily favors saved content, making postpartum practical content algorithmically advantaged

## 5. #HomeschoolTok / #HomeschoolMom

### Key Hashtags
- **#homeschool** -- 4.9B views, 495.1K posts
- **#homeschooltok** -- community-identity tag (subset of #homeschool)
- **#homeschoolmom** -- niche tag, connects with highly interested followers
- **#homeschooling**, **#homeschoollife**
- **#homeschoolcurriculum**, **#homeschooldayinthelife**

### Size & Scale
A substantial and growing community. The #homeschool umbrella at 4.9B views is significant, and the community has expanded notably since the 2020 pandemic when many families began homeschooling. The community is well-organized with clear sub-niches (curriculum reviews, daily routines, faith-based homeschooling, unschooling).

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Predominantly women (mothers) aged 28-45
- Heavily US-based with growing UK presence
- Two distinct demographic clusters:
  1. Faith-based homeschoolers (conservative, larger families, often single-income)
  2. Secular/progressive homeschoolers (flexible education philosophy, often dual-income with remote work)
- Family size skews larger than average (3+ children is common)
- Many are also RV/travel families or homesteaders

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- Day-in-the-life routines showing the homeschool schedule
- Curriculum hauls and reviews (back-to-school season is peak)
- Organization systems (binders, planners, schedules)
- "What we eat in a day" -- feeding kids who are home ALL DAY
- Morning basket / morning time routines
- Encouragement for overwhelmed homeschool parents

**Dominant themes:** Intentional living, curriculum choice anxiety, "you can do this," schedule flexibility, the myth vs. reality of homeschooling.

### Key Creators
| Creator | Handle | Followers | Notes |
|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| iamtraeh | @iamtraeh | ~817K | Top homeschool TikTok creator |
| itsme_lisap | @itsme_lisap | ~663K | Homeschool content creator |
| kt.gonzales | @kt.gonzales | ~532K | Homeschool routines and tips |
| lex.delarosa | @lex.delarosa | ~457K | Homeschool lifestyle |
| Christy Faith | (homeschool creator) | ~250K | Helps parents build successful homeschools |

### Meal Planning Relevance: HIGH
- Homeschool families must plan **3 full meals + snacks daily** because kids are home all day
- "Homeschool lunch ideas" is a dedicated content subcategory on TikTok
- Meal planning is discussed as part of the broader homeschool organization/scheduling conversation
- Parents report that meal interruptions disrupt learning time -- planning ahead is positioned as an educational strategy, not just a food strategy
- Content exists about kid-friendly lunches that children can prepare independently while parent teaches
- The "what we eat in a day" format naturally overlaps with meal planning content

### Competitive Presence
- **No meal planning apps** specifically targeting homeschool families
- Curriculum planning tools (homeschool planners) are common but don't include meals
- Lifestyle organization apps (Notion templates, Google Sheets) are used for meal planning alongside schooling
- Some meal delivery services appear but are positioned generically
- **Notable gap:** No tool connects the homeschool schedule with meal timing/planning

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** Peacekeeper -- homeschool moms carry the entire educational AND food system for the household. They are the definition of "carries the family food system."
- **Key Feature:** Weekly meal plans that cover all meals (not just dinner), though Slated's dinner focus may feel limiting. If Slated could position its dinner planning as "one meal handled, two to go," it still reduces cognitive load.
- **Secondary Feature:** Dinner Draft -- homeschool families often involve kids in decision-making as part of their educational philosophy. A voting feature aligns with the co-learning culture.
- **Angle:** "Homeschool the kids. Let us handle dinner." Position as complementary to the homeschool planning mindset.
- **Risk:** This community has strong Perfectionist overlap. Many homeschool creators emphasize scratch cooking, seasonal eating, and intentional food choices. Slated must not feel like a shortcut that compromises food quality.
- **Risk 2:** Faith-based segment may be less tech-forward.

### Engagement Quality
- Very high save rates (this community saves EVERYTHING -- planners, ideas, resources)
- Strong comment engagement (advice-seeking and sharing)
- Moderate share rates (content is shared within homeschool co-ops and groups)
- Seasonal spikes around back-to-school planning periods
- Educational content engagement rates on TikTok average 9.5%, well above platform norms

## 6. #LargeFamily / #MomOf4+

### Key Hashtags
- **#largefamily** -- estimated 500M-1B+ views (content from large families has 240M+ views in subsets)
- **#largefamilymeals** -- dedicated subtag
- **#momof4**, **#momof5**, **#momof6**, **#momof7**, **#momof8**, **#momof10**
- **#feedingalargefamily**, **#largefamilymealprep**
- **#familydinnerideas** -- 4B views (broader tag)
- **#mealprep** -- 21B views (broader tag, heavily used by large-family creators)

### Size & Scale
Large family content is one of the highest-performing sub-niches in all of parenting TikTok. While the specific #largefamily tag may be mid-sized, large-family creators cross-post into massive tags like #mealprep (21B views) and #familydinnerideas (4B views). Individual creators in this space regularly achieve millions of followers. The community is defined less by a single hashtag and more by a network of interconnected mega-creators.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Mix of parents with large families AND aspirational viewers who watch for entertainment
- Predominantly women aged 28-45
- US-based majority with strong following from UK, Australia
- Income varies widely but grocery spending is a constant topic
- Family sizes range from 4 children to 12+ children
- Many include adopted/foster children (overlap with blended family community)
- Religious families (Mormon, Catholic, evangelical) are well-represented but not exclusively

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- Mega meal prep videos ("feeding a family of 11 for the week")
- Grocery haul content with budget reveals
- "What's for dinner" serving videos showing plated meals for 6-12 people
- Morning/evening routines showing logistics of managing many children
- Before/after kitchen organization
- Viral "cooking for my family" videos (Dougherty Dozen, 7KidsKitchen)

**Dominant themes:** Logistics mastery, "how we make it work," feeding everyone on a budget, organized chaos, family togetherness at the dinner table.

**Warning for Slated:** Budget content is extremely dominant here. The Dougherty Dozen's $1,200/week grocery budget is a frequent topic. Budget Optimizer persona is very active. However, the *volume* challenge (cooking for 8-12 people nightly) transcends budget concerns and represents a genuine HVP pain point.

|---------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Alicia Dougherty (Dougherty Dozen) | @doughertydozen | ~7.7M | Mom of 10-11; $1,200/week groceries; viral meal prep; 17.9M views on single video |
| Mary Neilis (7KidsKitchen) | @7kidskitchen | Est. 500K+ | Mom of 7; realistic "healthy-ish" meals; went viral on TikTok + Substack |

### Meal Planning Relevance: EXTREMELY HIGH
- This is the community where **meal planning IS the content**
- Dougherty Dozen built a career around meal prep for large families
- 7KidsKitchen turned nightly dinners into a full-time content business
- "What's for dinner" videos are the dominant format
- Large-family creators actively discuss meal planning systems and tools
- Taco Tuesday, breakfast for dinner, and rotation-based meal systems are common
- The challenge of managing multiple dietary preferences within a large family is a constant topic
- Some creators have Substack newsletters extending their meal planning content

### Competitive Presence
- **No dedicated meal planning apps** targeting large families observed
- Grocery store brands (Walmart, Costco, Aldi) have massive organic presence through haul content
- Costco is essentially an unofficial sponsor of large-family content
- Meal kit services are generally NOT present (too expensive per-serving for large families and portions are too small)
- Some cookbook authors and Substack creators monetize meal plans
- **Opportunity:** Large families are underserved by existing meal planning apps because most apps don't scale well to 6-12+ servings

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** Both HVP and Peacekeeper are strongly represented
  - HVP: "I need a plan for 9 people that I can approve in 30 seconds"
  - Peacekeeper: "Everyone wants something different and I'm making one dinner"
- **Key Feature:** Dinner Draft is transformative here. With 4-10 children all wanting different things, a voting system prevents nightly negotiations.
- **Secondary Feature:** Scaling recipes to 6-12 servings; managing multiple dietary restrictions/allergies across many children
- **Angle:** "One dinner. Ten opinions. Zero arguments." Lean into the chaos-management angle.
- **Strategic note:** Partnership with a top large-family creator (Dougherty Dozen, 7KidsKitchen) could drive massive awareness. These creators have built their brands on meal content and are actively looking for tools and partnerships.
- **Risk:** Budget sensitivity is high. $7.99/month must be justified against the "I can plan meals myself" mentality. Frame as time savings at scale ("you save 30 minutes of planning per day x 365 = 182 hours/year").

### Engagement Quality
- Meal prep videos drive extremely high saves (viewers save for recipe replication)
- Grocery hauls drive high engagement across likes, comments, and shares
- "What's for dinner" serving videos are highly shareable
- Cross-platform amplification is strong (TikTok creators also have YouTube, Substack, Instagram)
- Brand partnership interest is very high among large-family creators

## 7. #CoParenting

### Key Hashtags
- **#coparenting** -- 84.5M posts on TikTok (very high post volume)
- **#coparent**, **#coparentinglife**
- **#coparentingtips**, **#coparentingwin**
- **#custodyschedule**, **#splitcustody**
- **#parallelparenting** (for high-conflict situations)

### Size & Scale
A large and active community with 84.5 million posts. View counts were not directly available but given the post volume, likely in the multi-billion range. The community overlaps significantly with #singlemomtok and #blendedfamily but has its own distinct identity focused on the *relationship between co-parents* rather than the parenting experience itself. A viral co-parenting video achieved 17M+ views, demonstrating significant reach potential.

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Both men and women, though women create more content (60-70% of creators)
- Aged 25-42
- Divorced/separated parents
- Often sharing custody on some form of alternating schedule
- US-based majority
- Mix of amicable and high-conflict co-parenting situations
- Many have remarried or repartnered (overlap with blended family)

### Content Culture
**What performs well:**
- "Co-parenting win" videos showing positive interactions between exes
- Custody exchange content (drop-offs and pick-ups)
- Advice for navigating high-conflict co-parenting
- Humor about coordinating with an ex
- "Things that helped me become a better co-parent" lists
- Holiday coordination content (who gets the kids for Thanksgiving/Christmas)

**Dominant themes:** "Doing it for the kids," rising above conflict, setting boundaries, communication strategies, parallel parenting for high-conflict situations, celebrating co-parenting wins.

|---------|--------|-----------|-------|

### Meal Planning Relevance: MODERATE-HIGH
- **The core meal-planning challenge is unique:** coordinating what kids eat across two separate households
- "What do you feed them at your house?" is a recurring source of conflict between co-parents
- Kids transitioning between homes often report "dad makes this, mom makes that" as a source of inconsistency
- Allergies and dietary restrictions must be communicated and maintained across two households
- Custody schedules mean variable household size (planning for 2 on weekdays, 4 on weekends, or vice versa)
- Meal planning is discussed less explicitly than in large-family or postpartum communities, but the *need* is implicit in the logistics content

### Competitive Presence
- **Co-parenting apps** are present: TalkingParents, OurFamilyWizard, AppClose -- but focused on communication and scheduling, NOT meals

- Some family organization tools (Cozi) are mentioned but not food-specific
- The space between co-parenting communication apps and meal planning apps is entirely unoccupied

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Primary Persona:** Peacekeeper -- "Your family's input before dinner. Not their complaints after." In co-parenting, the complaints often come from the other household.
- **Key Feature:** The ability to maintain consistent meal plans that work across two homes. If both co-parents used Slated, dietary restrictions and preferences would stay consistent regardless of which house the child is at.
- **Secondary Feature:** Dinner Draft could include kids' preferences that are consistent between homes, reducing the "I liked what we had at mom's/dad's house better" dynamic.
- **Angle:** "Same kid. Two kitchens. One plan." Position Slated as the consistency layer across households.
- **Advanced Angle:** Offer a "shared profile" feature where co-parents can both access a child's dietary preferences and allergy list, even if they maintain separate meal plans.
- **Risk:** High-conflict co-parents may not want ANY shared tool with their ex. Parallel parenting (zero communication) is a recognized approach. Slated would need to allow independent use while maintaining child preference data.
- **Opportunity:** Co-parenting coaches could be ideal micro-influencer partners -- they're already advising on logistics and tools.

### Engagement Quality
- Very high comment engagement (advice-seeking and debates)
- Strong save rates on tips/advice content
- Shares to ex-partners are common ("you should see this")
- Duets and stitches are frequent (creators respond to each other's co-parenting takes)
- Polarizing content (co-parenting debates) drives algorithm engagement through comments

### Competitive Landscape Summary

Across all 7 communities, **no dedicated meal planning app** was found with specific positioning or creator partnerships targeting any of these parenting life-stage communities. The competitive landscape consists of:

- **Meal kit delivery services** (HelloFresh, EveryPlate) -- present in general mom content but not targeted to specific life-stages
- **Meal delivery services** (Mom's Meals) -- only targeting postpartum, insurance-covered, not app-based
- **Co-parenting apps** (TalkingParents, OurFamilyWizard) -- adjacent but scheduling-focused, not meal-focused
- **Family organization apps** (Cozi, OurHome) -- broad family management, not meal planning specific
- **Grocery retailers** (Costco, Walmart, Aldi) -- dominant organic presence through haul content but not tools
- **DIY solutions** (Notion templates, Google Sheets, ChatGPT) -- mentioned by military spouses and homeschool parents
- **Cookbook authors / Substack creators** -- monetizing meal plans in large-family space

**Key Finding:** There is essentially zero competition from meal planning apps targeting these specific parenting life-stage communities on TikTok. This represents a significant first-mover opportunity for Slated.

5. **Some communities overlap significantly.** A military spouse who homeschools and has a blended family with 6 kids could appear in 3-4 of these communities simultaneously. Cross-community strategies may reach the same audiences through different identity lenses.
