## 1. Scoring Methodology & Rubrics

### Five Dimensions

Each community is scored 1-10 on five dimensions. Weights reflect what matters most for a new organic TikTok presence:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Persona Match (PM)** | 30% | How closely the community's audience maps to HVP or Peacekeeper personas. Does this community contain people who would pay $7.99/month for cognitive relief or family harmony? |
| **Pain Point Alignment (PPA)** | 25% | Whether the community explicitly discusses the problem Slated solves — dinner planning, meal decision fatigue, family food conflict. "Explicitly" matters: implicit adjacency scores lower than direct discussion. |
| **Competitive Vacancy (CV)** | 20% | Absence of meal planning apps (not meal kits, not recipe apps — meal PLANNING apps specifically). |
| **Engagement Quality (EQ)** | 15% | Does engagement indicate purchase intent? Saves and solution-seeking comments score higher than laughs and shares. Communities where people ask "what app do you use?" score higher than communities where people say "so relatable lol." |
| **Size & Reach (SR)** | 10% | Community size on TikTok. Deliberately low-weighted because TikTok's algorithm rewards niche authority over broad reach. A 500M-view community with 9/10 persona match beats a 100B-view community with 4/10 persona match. |

### Scoring Rubrics

**Persona Match (PM):**
- 10: Community IS the persona. Members describe Slated's exact pain without knowing Slated exists.
- 8-9: Strong overlap. Most members fit one or both personas.
- 6-7: Meaningful subset matches. Community center is adjacent.
- 4-5: Some overlap, but most members are outside target personas.
- 1-3: Minimal overlap. Community attracts exclusion personas (budget optimizers, perfectionists).

**Pain Point Alignment (PPA):**
- 10: Dinner/meal planning is THE central, repeatedly discussed pain point.
- 8-9: Meal planning is a frequently and explicitly discussed pain point (not the only one).
- 6-7: Food/meal challenges are discussed but not central to the community.
- 4-5: Adjacent pain points. Meal planning is implicit or secondary.
- 1-3: Community discussions don't meaningfully relate to dinner/meal planning.

**Competitive Vacancy (CV):**
- 10: Zero competitors of any kind. No meal planning apps, no meal kit brands, no food-tech.
- 8-9: No direct competitors. Some adjacent brands (meal kits, grocery delivery) have influencer presence.
- 6-7: One small/early direct competitor OR significant adjacent brand presence.
- 4-5: Multiple adjacent competitors or one established direct competitor.
- 1-3: Established competitors with active strategies.

**Engagement Quality (EQ):**
- 10: Community actively seeks and recommends tools/apps. High save rates. Comments ask for solutions.
- 8-9: Strong solution-seeking behavior. People share what works for them. High save/share ratios.
- 6-7: Mix of entertainment and utility. Moderate solution-seeking.
- 4-5: Primarily entertainment-driven engagement. Likes and laughs, not saves and solutions.
- 1-3: Low engagement or engagement that doesn't indicate purchase intent.

**Size & Reach (SR):**
- 10: 100B+ cumulative views
- 8-9: 10B-100B views
- 6-7: 1B-10B views
- 4-5: 100M-1B views
- 2-3: 10M-100M views
- 1: <10M views or effectively absent from TikTok

### Formula

**Weighted Score = (PM × 0.30) + (PPA × 0.25) + (CV × 0.20) + (EQ × 0.15) + (SR × 0.10)**

Maximum possible: 10.0 (displayed as /100 for readability)

## 3. What the Scores Reveal: Six Key Findings

### Finding 1: The highest-scoring communities are organized around PROBLEMS, not demographics.

The top 5 communities are: #MentalLoad (94.5), #ADHDTok (91.0), #FoodAllergyMom (89.5), #DinnerIdeas (89.0), and #PickyEater (88.0). None of these are defined by who people are. They're defined by what people struggle with.

This matters for strategy. TikTok's algorithm serves content to people based on interest signals, not demographics. A video about "the mental load of dinner planning" will reach people who engage with mental load content regardless of whether they're 28 or 42, working or stay-at-home, urban or suburban. Targeting pain points rather than demographics leverages how TikTok actually distributes content.

**Implication:** Lead with the problem in content, not the audience. "This is what dinner decision fatigue looks like" will reach the right people. "Hey working moms!" will reach only a slice of the right people.

### Finding 2: A "medical necessity" cluster emerged that the original report missed entirely.

Six communities where meal planning is not optional but medically necessary:

| Community | Score | Why Meal Planning Is Non-Optional |
|-----------|-------|----------------------------------|
| #FoodAllergyMom | 89.5 | Every meal is a life-safety check (anaphylaxis risk) |
| #AutismTok | 85.0 | 70% of autistic children have atypical eating; sensory-safe meals required |
| #CeliacTok | 82.0 | Every meal must be verified gluten-free; cross-contamination vigilance |
| #DiabetesTok | 80.5 | Every meal directly impacts blood sugar; wrong choice = dangerous spike |
| #ChronicIllnessTok | 80.0 | Specific diets (AIP, anti-inflammatory) + limited energy for planning |
| #ARFID | 78.5 | Clinical eating disorder with extremely limited food acceptance |

**Why this matters:** These communities have the highest theoretical conversion rates because the alternative to meal planning is worse health outcomes, not just inconvenience. They also have the highest theoretical retention because they can't "go back to winging it." Zero meal planning apps compete here.

**The risk:** Medical-necessity communities demand higher accuracy. If Slated recommends a recipe with hidden gluten to a celiac user, or includes an allergen for a food allergy family, the consequences are medical, not merely annoying. The product must earn trust through accuracy before marketing to these communities.

**Product implication:** Slated already handles 16 dietary restrictions and 12 allergy types. This infrastructure maps directly to these communities. The question is whether the accuracy bar is high enough for medical-stakes use cases.

### Finding 3: Competitive vacancy is nearly universal — it doesn't differentiate communities.

48 of 57 communities scored 8-10 on Competitive Vacancy. This means the competitive gap the original report emphasized as a differentiator is actually a constant. Almost NO meal planning app has a TikTok presence anywhere.

The exceptions (scoring <8 on CV):
- #CoParenting (5/10): Co-parenting apps with meal planning features exist
- #FoodTok (5/10): Dominated by meal kit and recipe brands
- #HealthyEating (6/10): Hungryroot heavily present
- #RecipeTok (7/10): Paprika benefits from organic UGC

**Implication:** "We have no competitors" is true virtually everywhere. It's not a reason to pick one community over another. The differentiating dimensions are Persona Match and Pain Point Alignment.

### Finding 4: The Peacekeeper persona has more high-scoring community pathways than the HVP.

Across Tier 1 communities:
- **Peacekeeper-primary:** MentalLoad, FoodAllergyMom, PickyEater, FamilyMeals, AutismTok, FairPlay (6 communities)
- **HVP-primary:** WorkingMom (1 community)
- **Both personas:** ADHDTok, DinnerIdeas (2 communities)

This doesn't mean the HVP is less important as a customer. It means the Peacekeeper's pain has more distinct communities where it's discussed. The HVP's pain (cognitive overload) is discussed inside communities like #WorkingMom and #CorporateTok, but it isn't the community's organizing principle the way emotional labor is for #MentalLoad.

**Implication:** Content strategy should lean Peacekeeper-first in community selection, because there are simply more high-scoring communities built around the Peacekeeper's pain. This doesn't mean HVP messaging is less effective — it means there are fewer dedicated HVP communities and more Peacekeeper communities. HVP content can still perform across communities like #ProductivityTok, #CorporateTok, and #ADHDTok.

### Finding 5: Several communities function as distribution channels, not pain-point communities.

Three entities in the research are strategically important but score poorly on the pain-point framework because they serve different functions:

| Entity | Framework Score | Actual Strategic Value | Function |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|----------|
| #MomInfluencer / #MomMustHaves | 62.0 | Very High | Distribution channel: how apps reach all mom communities |
| #AppTok / #TechTok | 58.0 | High | Discovery channel: "apps that changed my life" roundups |
| #DecisionFatigue | 85.0 (raw) | Critical | Content pillar: the psychological frame for all Slated messaging |

**Implication:** The scoring framework measures community targeting value, not total strategic value. Distribution channels and content pillars require separate strategic treatment.

### Finding 6: Life-stage communities have unique "always-on acquisition" properties.

Three communities have a structural advantage the scoring framework doesn't capture: they perpetually generate new members.

| Community | Score | Renewal Rate | Unique Property |
|-----------|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| #PostpartumTok | 78.5 | ~3.6M new US parents/year | Audience renews annually; evergreen content reaches fresh audiences |
| #NewMom / #FTM | 72.5 | Same cohort | Habit-formation moment: meal planning becomes necessary for the first time |
| #HomeschoolTok | 76.0 | Growing 6%/year post-pandemic | Long engagement window (K-12: 13 years per family) |

**Implication:** These communities are acquisition funnels with perpetual fresh audiences. A piece of content posted today will reach new parents 6 months from now because the community constantly renews. Invest in evergreen content for these communities.

## 4. Cluster Analysis: How Communities Group Naturally

The 57 communities don't exist in isolation. They cluster around shared pain points and shared audiences. Understanding the clusters matters because content created for one community within a cluster often performs across the entire cluster.

### Cluster A: "The Invisible Labor System" (Peacekeeper Core)

**Communities:** #MentalLoad (94.5), #FairPlay (86.0), #FamilyMeals (85.5), #MarriageTok (81.0)
**Shared pain:** The emotional weight of carrying the family food system alone, unrecognized and unappreciated.
**Shared audience:** Women 28-42 carrying disproportionate cognitive household labor.
**Activation feature:** Dinner Draft (makes invisible work visible, creates family buy-in).
**Content frame:** "The hardest part of dinner isn't cooking. It's everything before."
**Bridge creator:** KC Davis (@domesticblisters) — appears in 4 communities across this cluster.

### Cluster B: "The Daily Question" (Both Personas)

**Communities:** #DinnerIdeas (89.0), #EasyRecipes (77.5), #MealPlanning (78.5), #SundayReset (72.5)
**Shared pain:** The recurring question "what's for dinner?" and the cognitive cost of answering it.
**Shared audience:** Parents actively seeking dinner solutions.
**Activation feature:** Ready-Made Plan (answers the question before it's asked).
**Content frame:** "I haven't asked 'what's for dinner' in 3 months."
**Note:** This cluster is the most directly product-relevant. These people are already looking for what Slated does.

### Cluster C: "The Executive Function Gap" (HVP Core)

**Core communities:** #ADHDTok (91.0), #WorkingMom (87.0)
**Distribution opportunities (not core):** #CorporateTok (72.5), #ProductivityTok (71.5), #NurseTok (83.0) — these are professional identity communities where Slated content may resonate with a subset, but meal planning is not the organizing topic. NurseTok PPA is 7/10 (discussed but not central), CorporateTok PPA is 5/10 (barely comes up). They are places to reach the right people via hashtag targeting, not communities organized around the problem Slated solves.
**Shared pain:** Cognitive capacity is consumed by work/life demands; dinner planning competes with everything else.
**Shared audience:** Cognitively overloaded professionals, many with ADHD or ADHD-adjacent traits.
**Activation feature:** Ready-Made Plan (eliminates the cognitive cost of planning).
**Content frame:** "Your brain is full. Dinner shouldn't take up space in it."

### Cluster D: "The Medical Necessity" (Health-Condition Cluster)

**Communities:** #FoodAllergyMom (89.5), #AutismTok (85.0), #CeliacTok (82.0), #DiabetesTok (80.5), #ChronicIllnessTok (80.0), #ARFID (78.5)
**Shared pain:** Dietary restrictions make meal planning non-optional and cognitively expensive. Getting it wrong has health consequences.
**Shared audience:** People managing medical dietary needs for themselves or their children.
**Activation feature:** Dietary restriction engine (16 restrictions, 12 allergy types, 12 nutritional focus areas).
**Content frame:** "Every meal is safe. Every time. No checking."
**Note:** Highest theoretical conversion and retention. Highest accuracy requirements. Requires product validation before marketing entry.

### Cluster E: "The Family Configuration Challenge" (Peacekeeper Variants)

**Communities:** #PickyEater (88.0), #LargeFamily (77.5), #BlendedFamily (74.0), #HomeschoolTok (76.0), #GentleParenting (77.0)
**Shared pain:** Complex family preference sets. More people = more opinions = more negotiation.
**Shared audience:** Parents managing multiple preference sets (picky eaters, large families, step-families).
**Activation feature:** Dinner Draft (democratic meal selection across complex families).
**Content frame:** "My kids voted for dinner tonight. And they actually ate it."

### Cluster F: "The Life Transition" (Acquisition Funnel)

**Communities:** #PostpartumTok (78.5), #NewMom/FTM (72.5), #SingleMomTok (81.0)
**Shared pain:** Household system is being rebuilt from scratch. Meal planning transitions from optional to essential.
**Shared audience:** People entering a new life phase where cognitive load dramatically increases.
**Activation feature:** Ready-Made Plan (provides structure when everything else is chaos).
**Content frame:** "You have enough to figure out. Dinner's handled."
**Note:** These are acquisition moments. Capture users during transition, retain through life stage.

## 5. Strategic Analysis

### The Central Question

The original report asked: "Which communities should Slated target?" and answered it with a pre-selected list.

The right question is: "Given Slated's product, personas, and resources, what does the analysis tell us about where to invest and why?"

### What the Analysis Reveals

**1. Slated has two distinct strategic paths, and they require different content and features.**

Path A: **Cognitive relief** (HVP-primary). "The plan is ready. You just have to say yes."
- Best communities: WorkingMom, ADHDTok, NurseTok, CorporateTok, SingleMomTok
- Lead feature: Ready-Made Plan
- Content style: Efficiency, "hack," day-in-the-life integration
- Conversion mechanism: "Try it and see how much mental space you get back"

Path B: **Family harmony** (Peacekeeper-primary). "Everyone agreed. No complaints."
- Best communities: MentalLoad, PickyEater, FamilyMeals, FairPlay, GentleParenting, MarriageTok
- Lead feature: Dinner Draft (family voting)
- Content style: Emotional, relational, "before and after" family dynamics
- Conversion mechanism: "Show it to your family and watch what happens"

Both paths are valid. The analysis shows more Tier 1 communities along Path B (Peacekeeper), but Path A (HVP) has some of the largest individual communities (#ADHDTok at 39.7B views, #WorkingMom).

**2. The medical-necessity cluster is the highest-potential new opportunity — but requires product validation first.**

FoodAllergyMom (89.5) scores higher than WorkingMom (87.0). This is not an artifact of the scoring — it reflects genuine reality: a parent managing multiple food allergies has MORE intense meal planning pain than a busy professional. Meal planning is literally a safety operation for them.

But this cluster has a prerequisite: Slated's dietary restriction and allergy filtering must be verified for medical-grade accuracy. Marketing to food allergy families before the product can guarantee allergen-free recipes would be irresponsible and potentially dangerous.

**Recommendation:** Validate the accuracy of allergy/restriction filtering with clinical or dietitian review. Once validated, the medical-necessity cluster becomes Slated's highest-conversion opportunity.

**3. The original report's Tier 1 mostly holds, but with important additions and reorderings.**

Original Tier 1 (report's ranking):
1. MentalLoad — still #1 ✓
2. DinnerIdeas — drops to #4 (ADHDTok and FoodAllergyMom score higher)
3. WorkingMom — drops to #6
4. PickyEater — drops to #5
5. ADHDTok — rises to #2 (higher score than originally assigned)
6. FamilyMeals — stays at #8
7. EasyRecipes — drops out of Tier 1 entirely (77.5, now Tier 2)

New Tier 1 additions:
- #FoodAllergyMom (89.5) — was not in original report
- #FairPlay (86.0) — was Tier 2, now Tier 1
- #AutismTok (85.0) — was not in original report

**4. Content should be organized by cluster, not by individual community.**

A single piece of content about "the mental load of deciding what's for dinner" can perform across #MentalLoad, #FairPlay, #MarriageTok, and #FamilyMeals simultaneously. TikTok's algorithm distributes based on content signals, not hashtag targeting. Organizing content strategy around the 6 clusters is more efficient than creating community-specific content for 15+ communities.

**5. Distribution channels and content pillars require separate strategy from pain-point communities.**

Three elements don't fit the community-targeting framework but are strategically critical:

| Element | Strategy |
|---------|----------|
| **#MomInfluencer seeding** | Partner with 10-20 micro-influencers (50K-200K) for authentic "apps I use" content. This is the fastest path to initial TikTok awareness. Budget priority. |
| **"Dinner decision fatigue" as owned phrase** | Create a signature content series. Make this phrase synonymous with Slated on TikTok. This is the bridge between all clusters. |
| **Evergreen acquisition content for life-stage communities** | Create "welcome to parenthood — here's the one app that handles dinner" content that remains relevant as new parents cycle through #PostpartumTok and #NewMom. |

## 6. Account & Entry Strategy

### Multi-Account Approach: One Cluster Per Account

Slated will operate separate TikTok accounts per community cluster. This is driven by TikTok's 2026 algorithm:

- **Follower-first distribution:** Videos are tested on existing followers first. Mixed-cluster content on one account means followers attracted by one cluster's content may not engage with another cluster's content, killing Phase 1 distribution.
- **Niche authority:** The algorithm categorizes accounts by topic. Cluster-focused accounts build cleaner niche authority, leading to more precise audience matching.
- **Content production is automated.** The primary argument against multi-account (resource constraints from manual content creation) does not apply. The marginal cost per additional account is LLM calls for content generation.
- **TikTok Business Center supports up to 200 accounts** with role-based permissions — multi-account is an officially supported business practice.

Each account will focus on one cluster, with content repurposed across accounts using different hooks, positioning, and community-specific language.

### Starting Cluster: Invisible Labor (Peacekeeper Core)

**Account 1 communities:** #MentalLoad (94.5), #FairPlay (86.0), #FamilyMeals (85.5), #MarriageTok (81.0)

**Why start here:**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Score density** | Highest of any cluster. 3 Tier 1 communities + 1 strong Tier 2. Average score: 86.75 |
| **Pain point explicitness** | Dinner planning is THE central, explicit organizing topic in every community — not adjacent, not secondary. Creators in #MentalLoad name dinner planning as the #1 mental load task |
| **Within-cluster coherence** | All 4 communities share the same audience (women 28-42 carrying household cognitive labor), same emotional register, and same conversation. Content crosses naturally |
| **Lead feature** | Dinner Draft — unique to Slated, no competitor has it. Makes invisible work visible, creates family buy-in. Inherently visual and shareable |
| **Engagement quality** | High save/share rates — the signals the 2026 algorithm weights most. FairPlay users are already paying for household labor solutions |
| **Competitive vacancy** | Zero meal planning apps in any of these communities |

### Expansion Sequence

Once Account 1 is operational and producing consistent content:

**Account 2: Daily Question (Both Personas)**
- Communities: #DinnerIdeas (89.0), #MealPlanning (78.5), #EasyRecipes (77.5), #SundayReset (72.5)
- Most directly product-relevant — people actively searching for what Slated does
- Different content register (practical/solution-oriented vs. emotional/relational) — warrants separate account
- Core content from Account 1 can be repurposed with different hooks ("It's 4:47 and you still don't know what's for dinner" vs. "The hardest part of dinner isn't cooking")

**Account 3: Executive Function (HVP Core)**
- Core communities: #ADHDTok (91.0), #WorkingMom (87.0)
- Most distinct cluster — different audience, different language, different content style
- Distribution opportunities via hashtag targeting: #CorporateTok, #ProductivityTok, #NurseTok (professional identity communities where a subset matches the HVP persona, but meal planning is not the organizing topic)
- ADHDTok is massive (39.7B views) and explicitly discusses meal planning as an executive function struggle

**Future accounts (evaluate based on Account 1-3 performance data):**
- Family Configuration (#PickyEater, #LargeFamily, #BlendedFamily, #GentleParenting) — Dinner Draft is the lead feature
- Life Transition (#PostpartumTok, #SingleMomTok, #NewMom) — acquisition funnel with perpetual renewal
- Medical Necessity — only after product accuracy validation (see Section 7, Risk 1)

## 7. Risks, Tensions, and What Could Go Wrong

### Risk 1: Medical-necessity communities have medical-consequence stakes
If Slated recommends a recipe with hidden allergens or gluten, the trust damage is permanent and the health consequences are real. Do not enter these communities until the product's accuracy is validated.

### Risk 2: Some high-scoring communities have philosophical tensions
- **#GentleParenting** (77.0): Some members may view ANY automation as removing intentionality from family meals.
- **#MinimalismTok** (63.5): The "slow living" subset actively rejects efficiency tools.
- **#SAHM** (59.0): Some SAHMs view meal planning as their identity — automating it removes their purpose.

### Risk 3: Budget-sensitive communities need free-tier visibility
#SingleMomTok (81.0), #HomeschoolTok (76.0), #NurseTok (83.0), and #LargeFamily (77.5) all contain price-sensitive segments. Marketing without a visible free tier will generate comments like "must be nice to afford that" — which poisons the comment section.

### Risk 4: Cultural community entry requires authentic representation
#BlackMomTok and #LatinaMomTok score well (76.5) but entry must be led by creators from those communities, not by generic branded content. Slated's 20-cuisine architecture is a genuine strength, but it must be represented by people who actually cook those cuisines.

### Risk 5: The exclusion personas are active in adjacent communities
Budget optimizers dominate #GroceryHaul, #BudgetMeals, and parts of #LargeFamily. Food perfectionists dominate #RecipeTok, #FoodTok, and parts of #HealthyEating. Content in Tier 1-2 communities will inevitably reach these audiences. The messaging must be clear enough that exclusion personas self-select out ("this isn't for me") without feeling rejected.

## 8. Scoring Rationale for Top Communities

Brief rationale for each Tier 1 community's dimension scores:

### #MentalLoad / #InvisibleLabor (94.5)

- **PM 10/10:** This community IS the Peacekeeper persona. Creator @sheisapaigeturner literally describes Slated's value prop without knowing it exists.
- **PPA 10/10:** Dinner planning is the #1 cited mental load task in this community. A viral video about ChatGPT managing mental load got 710K+ views. The problem is named, discussed, and agonized over daily.
- **CV 10/10:** Zero meal planning apps. Zero food tech brands. Completely uncontested.
- **EQ 9/10:** Extremely high save/share rates. Comment sections function as solution-seeking support groups. Community actively asks "what helps with this?"
- **SR 6/10:** 2-5B estimated cumulative views. Medium-sized but high virality potential (individual videos regularly hit 500K-5M).

### #ADHDTok (91.0)

- **PM 9/10:** Executive function struggles map directly to both personas. ADHD makes the cognitive chain of meal planning (recipe selection → preference management → shopping list → execution) genuinely dysfunctional.
- **PPA 9/10:** Meal planning is one of the most commonly cited executive function struggles. Creators share "meal prep for brains that hate meal prep." Community actively seeks app recommendations.
- **CV 10/10:** No meal planning app targets ADHDTok despite near-perfect fit.
- **EQ 9/10:** Community actively recommends tools and systems. Extremely high save rates on "things that help my ADHD." Comments frequently ask "what app is this?"
- **SR 8/10:** 39.7B cumulative views. 7th most popular health hashtag on TikTok. Massive and growing.

### #FoodAllergyMom (89.5)

- **PM 9/10:** These are Peacekeeper persona at maximum intensity. Every meal is a safety operation. Managing multiple allergens (peanut, tree nut, milk, egg, wheat, soy, fish, shellfish, sesame) for one or more children while feeding the rest of the family.
- **PPA 10/10:** Meal planning IS this community's central activity. Every day, every meal. The cognitive load of checking every ingredient, every label, every recipe is the defining challenge.
- **CV 10/10:** No meal planning app targets food allergy families. Spokin and Fig are allergy product/restaurant finders, not meal planners.
- **EQ 9/10:** Exceptionally high save rates on every safe recipe. Allergy families bookmark obsessively. Comments verify safety. These users would pay IMMEDIATELY for verified-safe meal plans.
- **SR 4/10:** ~500M-1B estimated views. Smaller community. But highest-intent users on TikTok — every member has acute, daily need.

### #DinnerIdeas / #WhatsForDinner (89.0)

- **PM 9/10:** Community exists because of the exact question Slated answers. Both personas are here: HVP ("just tell me what to make") and Peacekeeper ("something everyone will eat").
- **PPA 10/10:** The community IS the pain point. "What's for dinner?" is the organizing question. 1.8M+ posts, all variations on "help me decide."
- **CV 9/10:** Only Ollie (@heyollieai) has a tiny presence. Jow sponsors some creators. Otherwise empty.
- **EQ 8/10:** High save rates (bookmarking ideas). Comments often ask for apps/tools. But some engagement is purely entertainment (watching cooking videos).
- **SR 7/10:** 515M+ views for #DinnerIdeas specifically. Larger with adjacent hashtags.

### #PickyEater / #KidFriendlyMeals (88.0)

- **PM 9/10:** Near-perfect Peacekeeper match. The daily anxiety of "will they eat this?" + the family conflict when they don't.
- **PPA 9/10:** Feeding picky children IS the community's purpose. Not just discussed — it's the reason the community exists.
- **CV 10/10:** No meal planning app addresses this. Zero competition.
- **EQ 9/10:** Very high save rates. Parents bookmark every recipe that worked for someone else's picky child. Strong solution-seeking behavior.
- **SR 5/10:** Active but not massive by TikTok standards. @hangry_helper gained 100K+ rapidly, showing growth potential.

### #WorkingMom (87.0)

- **PM 10/10:** This IS the HVP persona. Dual-income, cognitively overloaded, already paying for convenience services.
- **PPA 8/10:** Dinner planning is frequently discussed as one of many stressors. Not THE organizing pain point (that's work-life balance broadly), but explicitly and regularly cited.
- **CV 9/10:** Jow has some presence. No other meal planning apps.
- **EQ 8/10:** Vulnerable, solution-seeking content. Creator-developed content delivers 70% higher CTR. But some engagement is solidarity/venting, not solution-seeking.
- **SR 7/10:** 40+ major influencers tracked. Substantial niche. Growing rapidly.

### #FairPlay (86.0)

- **PM 9/10:** Community uses systems to address household labor inequity. Already solution-oriented, already paying for tools (Fair Play cards: $22, workshops: more).
- **PPA 9/10:** The "Meals" card is one of the 100 Fair Play household tasks. Meal planning is explicitly discussed as a task to redistribute.
- **CV 10/10:** Completely uncontested. No food tech or meal planning brand present.
- **EQ 9/10:** Highest intent per user. Already paying for solutions. Already using frameworks. 8-12% engagement for niche content.
- **SR 3/10:** Small community. Niche. But highest conversion potential per user of any community researched.

### #FamilyMeals / #FamilyDinner (85.5)

- **PM 9/10:** Peacekeeper core. "Feeding my family" as act of love/service — and the frustration when that effort goes unappreciated.
- **PPA 9/10:** "Dinners I made for my family this week" and "nobody appreciates what I make" are recurring themes. Meal planning is embedded in the community's daily rhythm.
- **CV 9/10:** HelloFresh via influencers only. No meal planning apps.
- **EQ 8/10:** Strong save rates on weekly dinner roundup content. Family-of-9 content (@7kidskitchen7) goes viral. Comment sections share tips.
- **SR 6/10:** Active with weekend peaks. Not individually massive but strong when combined with adjacent tags.

### #AutismTok / #SensoryProcessing (85.0)

- **PM 8/10:** Extreme Peacekeeper variant. Parents of autistic children spend enormous cognitive energy on meals that won't trigger sensory distress. Autistic adults managing their own sensory needs also fit.
- **PPA 8/10:** ~70% of autistic children have atypical eating. "Safe foods," sensory-driven picky eating, and mealtime anxiety are core community topics. Slightly lower than PickyEater because not every AutismTok post is about food.
- **CV 10/10:** No meal planning app targets this community. Completely uncontested.
- **EQ 8/10:** Strong identification-driven engagement. Community actively recommends tools and shares what works. High save rates on food-related posts.
- **SR 9/10:** 50B+ cumulative views across autism ecosystem. One of TikTok's largest health communities.
