## Day 0: Pre-Launch Setup

Complete ALL of the following before any browsing or engagement begins. Do not skip any item.

### Username

- **Primary choice:** `@slateddinner`
- **Backup choices:** `@dinnerdone.app`, `@slated.app`, `@dinnerdoneslated`
- **Rules:** No numbers, no underscores if avoidable. Keep it under 15 characters. It must be immediately readable and brandable. The username should signal "dinner" or the brand name, NOT "mental load" -- you want to be the *solution*, not the *problem*.

### Profile Photo

- Use the Slated logo on a clean, solid-color background (warm terracotta, sage green, or off-white from the brand palette).
- Crop to center. Resolution must be at least 200x200px. TikTok displays it as a circle, so ensure the logo has breathing room on all sides.
- Do NOT use a stock photo, a face, or a photo of food. The logo establishes brand identity from day one.

### Bio (80-120 Characters)

The bio must accomplish three things: (1) signal the niche, (2) communicate the value prop, (3) include a soft CTA.

**Recommended bio:**
```
Dinner planning that ends the "what's for dinner?" fight.
Smart meal plans your whole family votes on.
Dinner, Done. Link below.
```

**Alternative:**
```
The app that takes dinner off your plate (and puts it on theirs).
Weekly meal plans. Family votes. Zero mental load.
```

**Key phrases to include** (these act as SEO keywords on TikTok):
- "dinner planning" or "meal planning"
- "mental load" or "what's for dinner"
- "family" or "whole family"

### Account Type

- **Switch to a Business Account immediately.** This unlocks the website link field, analytics, and the commercial music library. Go to Settings > Account > Switch to Business Account > Select "Food & Beverage" as industry.

### Link in Bio

- **Day 0-7:** Use a Linktree or Beacons page. Even before the app has a dedicated landing page, you want the link active. Include:
  - Link 1: App Store / waitlist / landing page
  - Link 2: "What is Slated?" (brief explainer or blog post)
  - Link 3: Instagram handle (if cross-promoting)
- **Important:** TikTok Business accounts get the website field immediately (no 1,000-follower requirement). Paste your Linktree URL directly.
- Update the link whenever you have a new CTA (e.g., when you launch a specific promo or content series).

## Days 1-3: Pure Consumption & Algorithm Training

**Goal:** Train the TikTok algorithm to understand that this account belongs in the Invisible Labor / Family Meals / MarriageTok niche. Every single action you take teaches the algorithm what type of creator you are.

**Success indicator:** By the end of Day 3, at least 60-70% of your For You Page (FYP) should show content related to mental load, family meals, parenting struggles, marriage dynamics, or household labor.

---

### Day 1: Initial Algorithm Training

**Session 1 -- Morning (15-20 minutes)**

1. Open TikTok. Do NOT go to the Search tab yet. Start on your For You Page.
2. Scroll past any content that is NOT related to your niche. Do not stop scrolling on it. Do not watch more than 1-2 seconds of irrelevant content.
3. When you see anything related to parenting, cooking, family life, marriage, or household management -- STOP. Watch the entire video. If it is under 30 seconds, watch it twice.
4. Use the Search bar to search these hashtags one at a time. Spend 2-3 minutes browsing each:
   - `#mentalload`
   - `#mentalloadofmotherhood`
   - `#defaultparent`
   - `#invisiblelabor`
   - `#fairplay`
5. Like 3-5 videos total from these searches. Pick the ones with the highest engagement (most comments, most shares).
6. Save 2 videos to your Favorites.
7. **Do NOT comment yet.** Day 1 is observation only.
8. **Do NOT follow anyone yet.**

**Session 2 -- Evening (15-20 minutes)**

1. Return to the For You Page. Notice if the content has already started shifting toward your niche.
2. Search these additional hashtags:
   - `#marriagetok`
   - `#familymeals`
   - `#familydinner`
   - `#whatsfordinner`
   - `#dinnerplanning`
3. Like 3-5 more videos (niche-relevant only).
4. Save 2-3 more videos.
5. Watch any video that resonates for the FULL duration. Replay strong ones.
6. **Total Day 1 engagement:** 6-10 likes, 4-5 saves, 0 comments, 0 follows.

**Goal:** Publish your first 4-6 pieces of content while maintaining strong engagement patterns. Establish the content pillars that will define this account.

### Day 4: First Post

**Morning Engagement (15 minutes)**
- FYP scroll and like 8-10 niche videos.
- Leave 3-4 comments.
- Save 2-3 videos.
- Follow 2-3 new niche creators.

**First Post -- Publish between 6:00-7:30 PM EST**

Why this time: The target demographic (women 28-42, likely parents) are in the post-dinner / kids-in-bed window. Data from Buffer's analysis of 7 million posts shows weekday evenings (5-9 PM) as peak engagement for parent-focused content.

**Content Type for Post 1: Text-overlay video OR carousel (photo slideshow)**

A carousel is strongly recommended for the first post because:
- TikTok is actively boosting carousels in 2025-2026 (newer format = algorithmic preference)
- Carousels generate higher dwell time (viewers swipe through at their own pace)
- They are easier to produce than video for a brand just getting started
- They work exceptionally well for "list" and "relatable content" formats

**Post 1 Concept: "5 Things the Default Parent Thinks About Before 8 AM"**

Carousel slides (5-7 slides):
1. Hook slide: "What's already running through the default parent's brain before 8 AM" (bold text, clean design)
2. "What everyone is eating for dinner tonight -- and whether there's enough chicken thawed"
3. "Who has a lunch account that needs refilling"
4. "Whether the toddler's shoes from last month still fit"
5. "That the pediatrician appointment needs to be rescheduled because it conflicts with picture day"
6. "The fact that nobody else in the house is thinking about ANY of this"
7. CTA slide: "If this is you, follow for your sanity. We made an app for this."

**Post details:**
- Add background music: Use a trending sound that fits the mood (melancholy, reflective, or humorous). Check the "Trending" sounds tab.
- **Hashtags (max 5 per TikTok's 2025 update):**
  - `#mentalload` (niche specific)
  - `#defaultparent` (niche specific)
  - `#momlife` (broad reach)
  - `#whatsfordinner` (content specific)
  - `#slateddinner` (branded)
- **Caption:** "The mental load starts before the coffee. What would you add to this list?"
- **Pinned comment:** Post a comment on your own video within 1 minute: "Seriously though -- what's the one that hits hardest for you?"

**Post-publish actions (critical first hour):**
- Stay on TikTok for 30-45 minutes after posting.
- Reply to EVERY comment within the first hour. Speed of reply matters enormously.
- Continue engaging with other creators' content between checking your post.

## Engagement Rules & Comment Playbook

### The Hierarchy of Algorithm Signals

Each action you take sends a signal to TikTok about your account and the content you interact with. Here is the ranking from strongest to weakest signal:

1. **Shares** (strongest signal) -- Tells TikTok "this content is so valuable I want others to see it." Sharing sends the strongest quality signal.
2. **Saves** -- Tells TikTok "this content has lasting value I want to return to." Saves indicate high value and intent to return.
3. **Comments** -- Tells TikTok "this content sparked a conversation." Quality of comment matters -- substantive comments carry more weight than emoji-only.
4. **Watch time / Completion** -- Tells TikTok "this content held attention." Watching a full video (or rewatching) is a powerful passive signal.
5. **Likes** -- The weakest active signal, but still important for establishing niche preferences. Think of likes as "votes" for what category you belong in.
6. **Follows** -- Tells TikTok "I want more content like this person creates." Important for niche training but should be used deliberately.

### Daily Engagement Quotas During Warmup

| Action | Days 1-3 | Days 4-7 | Days 8-14 |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|
| FYP browsing time | 30-40 min/day | 30-35 min/day | 25-30 min/day |
| Likes | 8-20/day | 10-18/day | 12-20/day |
| Comments on others' posts | 0-8/day | 4-8/day | 5-10/day |
| Saves | 4-7/day | 3-5/day | 3-5/day |
| Shares | 0-2/day | 1-3/day | 2-4/day |
| New follows | 0-8/day | 3-5/day | 2-4/day |
| Replies to comments on own posts | N/A | ALL within 1 hour | ALL within 1 hour |

**Pacing rules:**
- Wait at least 30 seconds between likes.
- Wait at least 2-3 minutes between comments.
- Do NOT engage in bursts. Spread actions across the session.
- Never exceed 50 likes in a single hour.
- Never follow more than 20 accounts in a single day.
- Never leave more than 15 comments in a single day during warmup.

### The Comment Playbook

**NEVER write these kinds of comments:**
- "Great video!" / "Love this!" / "So true!"
- "Follow for follow" / "Check out my page"
- Any emoji-only comment (heart, fire, 100)
- Any comment that is under 5 words
- Any comment that is generic enough to apply to ANY video

**ALWAYS write comments that:**
- Add a personal experience or anecdote related to the video
- Ask a follow-up question that extends the conversation
- Tag the specific thing in the video that resonated
- Offer a complementary perspective (not contradictory)
- Are 15-50 words long (enough substance to be meaningful, not so long they look like spam)

### Comment Templates by Context

**On a mental load video:**
- "The part about [specific detail from video] -- I literally had this exact moment last Tuesday. Nobody talks about how the PLANNING is the exhausting part, not the doing."
- "This is why I've started telling my partner the specific thing I need instead of 'can you help.' Changed everything. Still exhausting though."
- "The dinner version of this is a whole separate TED talk. You spend 20 minutes deciding what to cook before you even start cooking."

**On a family dinner / meal planning video:**
- "This is exactly the kind of dinner I aspire to but by 5 PM my brain has already made 400 decisions and I can't make one more."
- "The real recipe is 'figure out what everyone will eat without anyone complaining.' That's the hard part."
- "I love that this feeds the whole family -- the dietary restriction juggle is so real when you have picky eaters AND allergies."

**On a marriage / relationship video:**
- "The 'what's for dinner' text at 4 PM is genuinely the most loaded question in any marriage. It's never just about dinner."
- "My partner started taking over dinner planning on Wednesdays and it was the single biggest thing that helped our dynamic. Small thing, huge impact."
- "This is the conversation I've been trying to have but couldn't put into words. Sending this to my partner."

**On a Fair Play / equal partnership video:**
- "We tried the Fair Play cards and the dinner/meal planning section alone had like 8 sub-tasks neither of us had ever articulated."
- "The conceive-plan-execute framework changed how we talk about household labor. 'I cooked dinner' doesn't count if someone else planned the menu, checked the fridge, and made the grocery list."

### Should You Follow These Creators?

**Yes, but strategically:**
- Follow all Tier 1 creators (5 accounts) by end of Day 2.
- Follow Tier 2-3 creators (6-8 accounts) across Days 3-7.
- Follow Tier 4 and new discoveries across Days 8-14.
- **Total follows in 14 days: 25-40 accounts.** Keep it under 50.
- Your following list IS a signal. A new account following 500 random accounts looks like a bot. A new account following 30-40 creators in a tight niche looks intentional.

### Should You Like Their Videos?

**Yes, generously but not robotically:**
- Like every video you watch fully that is niche-relevant.
- Do NOT like videos you only watched 2-3 seconds of.
- Do NOT like videos outside your niche (this dilutes your algorithm training).
- Aim for 10-20 likes per day, spread across your browsing sessions.

### Should You Save Their Videos?

**Yes, this is critical:**
- Save the BEST videos -- the ones that are most aligned with Slated's content pillars.
- Save videos you might want to Stitch or Duet with later.
- Save videos that use formats you want to replicate.
- Saves are the second-strongest algorithm signal. Use them deliberately.
- Aim for 3-7 saves per day.

## What to Avoid During Warmup

### Actions That Trigger Bot Detection

| Behavior | Risk Level | Why It's Flagged |
|----------|------------|------------------|
| Liking more than 50 posts per hour | HIGH | Exceeds human behavior patterns |
| Following/unfollowing more than 30 accounts in a day | HIGH | Classic bot pattern |
| Commenting faster than once per minute | HIGH | Impossible for genuine engagement |
| Leaving identical or near-identical comments | HIGH | TikTok's AI detects copy-paste patterns |
| Using third-party automation tools | CRITICAL | Can result in permanent ban |
| Logging in from multiple IP addresses/devices | MEDIUM | Looks like account sharing or bot network |
| Mass-engaging immediately after account creation | HIGH | No human creates an account and instantly likes 100 posts |
| Using VPN or proxy | MEDIUM | Geo-inconsistency flag |

### Content Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---------|--------------|
| Posting on Day 0 or Day 1 | Algorithm hasn't categorized you yet; your content will be shown to random users |
| Posting more than 2x/day before Day 8 | Looks like spam from a new account |
| Using all 5 hashtags as broad/trending (e.g., #fyp, #viral) | Sends mixed signals; algorithm can't niche you |
| Using #fyp, #foryou, #foryoupage | These do nothing for distribution and waste a hashtag slot |
| Posting content outside your niche | Confuses the algorithm about what your account is about |
| Deleting and reposting videos | Can trigger spam flags |
| Posting with no caption | Wastes a major SEO/context signal |
| Using copyrighted music (on a Business Account) | Business accounts must use the Commercial Music Library |
| Posting landscape/horizontal video | TikTok is vertical-first; horizontal content gets penalized in distribution |

### Engagement Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---------|--------------|
| Watching random content (sports, memes, politics) during warmup | Dilutes your niche signal; algorithm gets confused |
| Engaging with content outside your niche communities | Same as above -- every like and comment trains the algorithm |
| Following celebrities, brands, or accounts outside your niche | Pollutes your "Following" signal |
| Not replying to comments on your own posts | Kills conversation momentum; algorithm deprioritizes |
| Replying to comments with generic "thanks!" | Adds no value; looks like a bot |
| Engaging only with mega-creators (1M+ followers) | Your comments get buried. Mix in smaller creators where your comments are more visible |
| Scrolling without interacting | Passive scrolling teaches the algorithm less than active engagement |

### Platform Policy Violations to Absolutely Avoid

- Never mention pricing ($7.99/month) in content during warmup. It feels salesy.
- Never include a direct "download now" CTA in the first 7 days. Build community first.
- Never make medical or health claims about meal planning.
- Never disparage partners/spouses in a mean-spirited way. Humor is fine; cruelty is not. The brand voice is empathetic, not hostile.
- Never use someone else's content without Stitch/Duet (which gives credit). Reposting is theft.
