# TikTok Community Research: Health Conditions & Neurodivergent Audiences
## Slated Dinner Planning App -- Market Opportunity Analysis
### Research Date: 2026-02-28

---

## Research Methodology

Data gathered via web search across TikTok hashtag analytics platforms, academic research papers, influencer databases (Feedspot, Collabstr, Social Cat, IZEA, Favikon), trade publications (PR Week, MM+M Online), and community sources. Where exact current metrics were unavailable, ranges and qualitative assessments are noted. TikTok hashtag view counts fluctuate and are point-in-time estimates.

## 1. #AutismTok / #SensoryProcessing

### Community Name & Key Hashtags
- Primary: #AutismTok, #SensoryProcessing, #ActuallyAutistic
- Food-specific: #AutismEating, #SensoryFood, #SafeFoods, #AutismFoodProblems, #AutismFriendlyPlates, #AutismPickyEater
- Adjacent: #Neurodivergent, #Neurospicy, #AutismMum, #SPD

### Size
- **#AutismTok / top autism hashtags**: Up to 5.26 billion cumulative views, 9.5 million likes across top content (per academic analysis of top autism hashtags)
- **#AutismFoodProblems**: Nearly 1 million views as of mid-2023, likely significantly higher now
- **Creator pool**: Extensive -- Feedspot tracks 60+ dedicated autism TikTok influencers; the broader neurodivergent creator ecosystem numbers in the hundreds
- Self-advocates represent 61.8% of creators; identity-first language used in 80.8% of content

### Core Audience & Demographics
- **Autistic adults** (self-advocates) are the primary creators and consumers
- **Parents of autistic children** form a major secondary audience, particularly around food/eating content
- She/they pronouns used by 26.6% of creators, indicating a younger, LGBTQ+-adjacent audience
- Skews female/nonbinary, ages 18-35
- Significant overlap with ADHD community (AuDHD content is rapidly growing)

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: Lived experience narratives (75.7% of content), psychoeducation, sensory sensitivity exploration, masking/unmasking, late diagnosis stories
- **What performs well**: Personal storytelling with emotional resonance; "day in my life" formats; safe food showcases; sensory overwhelm demonstrations; myth-busting content about autism stereotypes
- **Tone**: Identity-affirming, community-building, mixed positive/negative valence (84% of content). Anti-ableist language dominates (70.6%). Rejection of "deficit" framing
- **Engagement pattern**: High save rates on educational and resource content; shares on relatable lived experience content; comments are community-oriented and supportive

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **Paige Layle** (@paigelayle) -- 2.7M followers. Canadian autism/ADHD acceptance activist and author. Known for viral stereotype-busting videos (5M+ likes on top posts)
2. **My Brother Max** -- 1.2M followers. Sibling perspective on autism
3. **Chloe Hayden** -- 1.1M followers. Australian autistic actress and advocate
4. **Captain Autism Comedy** (AJ Wilkerson) -- 1.1M followers. Comedy-driven autism content
5. **@autismdietitian** (Brittyn) -- Specialist dietitian for children on the autism spectrum, creating content on expanding diets while honoring sensory needs

### Meal Planning Relevance
**HIGH.** Food is one of the most discussed subtopics within AutismTok. Specific meal planning pain points include:
- Sensory-driven food restriction (texture, temperature, smell, appearance)
- "Safe food" rotations that become nutritionally limiting
- Executive function challenges making meal planning overwhelming
- Parents struggling to feed autistic children who have extremely limited food acceptance
- A 2025 academic study confirmed TikTok is "the primary social media site through which autistic people are discussing issues with food and eating"
- Over 90% of autistic children and adults have sensory sensitivities affecting food choices

### Competitive Presence
- **Apps**: Only 2 apps out of 300+ screened specifically address autism feeding challenges (Autism Food Coach 2, EduKitchen). Massive gap in the market
- **General meal planning apps** (Mealime, Paprika, Yummly) are occasionally mentioned but not tailored to sensory needs
- **No dominant meal planning app** has established presence in this community
- Brands like ABA therapy companies and sensory product lines are active, but food tech is largely absent

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: Both HVP and Peacekeeper are highly relevant. Parents of autistic children carrying the cognitive load of managing safe food rotations are textbook Peacekeepers. Dual-income families paying for therapy and support services are HVPs
- **Key feature angle**: Sensory-aware meal planning -- ability to filter by texture, temperature, and ingredient familiarity. "Dinner Draft" could be adapted as a visual "safe food selector" for family members with sensory restrictions
- **Positioning**: "Your family's food system, built around what everyone can actually eat." Emphasize that Slated handles 16 dietary restrictions and can accommodate sensory-driven food preferences
- **Entry angle**: Partner with autism dietitians and parent advocates who already discuss meal planning struggles. Content showing Slated accommodating a family where one child has a 5-food rotation would resonate powerfully

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Saves are disproportionately high on educational and practical content (safe food lists, sensory accommodation tips)
- Comments are deeply engaged -- personal story sharing, community support, resource requests
- This community is skeptical of brands that don't demonstrate genuine understanding. Authenticity is non-negotiable
- Content created BY autistic people performs dramatically better than content ABOUT autistic people

## 2. #AnxietyTok / #TherapyTok

### Community Name & Key Hashtags
- Primary: #AnxietyTok, #TherapyTok, #MentalHealth
- Adjacent: #DecisionFatigue, #ExecutiveDysfunction, #SelfCare, #MentalHealthAwareness, #ADHD
- Meal-relevant: #WhatForDinner, #MealPlanHacks, #DinnerIdeas

### Size
- **#MentalHealth**: 25.3 billion views (as of 2022 measurement; significantly higher now), with 26.9 million posts under #MentalHealthAwareness
- **#SelfCare**: 95 million posts
- **#Anxiety / #AnxietyTok**: Over 1 billion views each
- Anxiety content represents 57% of all mental health videos regardless of search hashtag
- This is one of TikTok's largest content verticals, rivaling #Sports in post volume

### Core Audience & Demographics
- Over two-thirds of mental health content creators are female
- Gen Z is the dominant demographic, particularly proactive about wellness
- Ages 18-34 primary, with significant 35-44 parent audience
- Americans facing mental health professional shortages increasingly turn to TikTok for coping strategies
- Broad socioeconomic spread, but skews toward the educated/aware

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: Anxiety management strategies, therapy normalization, relationship dynamics, coping mechanisms, ADHD/executive function struggles, burnout/overwhelm
- **What performs well**: Therapist-style monologues ("Let me tell you something your therapist wants you to know..."), relatable humor about daily overwhelm, "name one thing that's not stressful right now" challenges, decision fatigue content
- **Tone**: Validating, warm, slightly clinical. Licensed professionals and peer advocates coexist. Comedy is a major vehicle
- **Key insight**: Creators have specifically identified "the five million decisions BEFORE making dinner" and "thinking through dinner EVERY DAY FOREVER" as resonant pain points

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **Elyse Myers** -- ~5M followers. Comedy + mental health destigmatization
2. **Dr. Julie Smith** -- 3.3M followers. Breaking down anxiety vs. panic attacks, practical psychology
3. **Matthias J. Barker** -- 2.5M followers. Psychotherapist creating "feel like you're in a therapist's office" monologue content
4. **Jeff Guenther** (TherapyDen founder) -- 1M+ followers. Relationship/dating/mental health advice
5. **Shani Tran** -- ~500K followers. Licensed counselor making therapy relatable

### Meal Planning Relevance
**MODERATE-HIGH.** The direct intersection of anxiety/therapy content and meal planning is emerging but not yet dominant. Key signals:
- Decision fatigue is a MAJOR theme -- and "what's for dinner?" is the universally recognized daily decision fatigue trigger
- Creator @karissaathome posted: "If trying to think of another dinner idea sends you straight into fight or flight, hi. I made something for us." This framing directly mirrors Slated's value proposition
- The neurodivergent meal planning subgenre (ADHD + anxiety) explicitly addresses executive function failures around dinner planning
- "Brain-off" go-to meals, "don't assign meals to specific days," and gamification strategies are popular content formats

### Competitive Presence
- **Low direct competition from meal planning apps** in this space
- Therapy apps (BetterHelp, Talkiatry) are heavy advertisers in TherapyTok
- Some general meal planning apps (Mealime, Paprika) appear in adjacent content but none specifically market to the anxiety/decision-fatigue angle
- "Anti-planning" planners and printable systems (Etsy-style) get organic traction

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: HVP is the primary target. The person who says "I don't want to build a plan. I want to sign off on one" is experiencing exactly the decision fatigue that AnxietyTok articulates daily
- **Key feature angle**: The pre-generated weekly meal plan eliminates the paralyzing "what's for dinner" decision entirely. This is anxiety reduction as a product feature
- **Positioning**: "Stop making dinner the hardest decision of your day." or "One less thing your brain has to figure out."
- **Entry angle**: Partner with mid-tier mental health creators (100K-500K range) who already make content about daily overwhelm. The "decision fatigue" → "here's an app that makes the decision for you" pipeline is natural and doesn't feel salesy. Dinner Draft feature positions family input BEFORE the meal, reducing the emotional labor of fielding complaints

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Shares are extremely high on relatable anxiety content ("tag someone who needs this")
- Saves are high on coping strategy and practical tip content
- This audience is receptive to solutions that reduce cognitive load -- they are LOOKING for tools
- Caution: avoid pathologizing normal stress. Position Slated as practical, not therapeutic

## 3. #DiabetesTok

### Community Name & Key Hashtags
- Primary: #DiabetesTok, #Diabetes, #T1D, #Type1Diabetes, #Type2Diabetes
- Specific: #T1DTips, #T1DCoach, #T1DWarrior, #DiabetesMealPlan, #BloodSugarManagement
- Food-related: #FoodForDiabetics, #DiabeticFriendlyRecipes, #DiabeticMealPrep

### Size
- **#Diabetes**: 16+ billion views, making it one of the largest health condition hashtags on TikTok
- **Audience composition**: 38% of the audience is ages 18-24
- Top five hashtags: #t1d, #t1diabetes, #type1diabetes, #t1dtips, #t1dcoach
- Robust creator ecosystem with 40+ dedicated diabetes TikTok influencers tracked by Feedspot (2026 list)

### Core Audience & Demographics
- **Type 1 diabetes**: Skews younger (diagnosed in childhood/adolescence), 18-24 core demo on TikTok
- **Type 2 diabetes**: Growing presence, slightly older demo (25-45)
- Parents of diabetic children form a significant audience segment
- Gender relatively balanced compared to other health communities
- Higher health literacy and strong interest in actionable medical information

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitor) data sharing, "what I eat in a day" with blood sugar impact, insulin management, device reviews, diagnosis stories, diabetes burnout, meal prep for blood sugar stability
- **What performs well**: Before/after blood sugar readings with specific meals; CGM footage overlaid on daily life; "foods that don't spike my blood sugar"; relatable frustration content about managing a 24/7 condition
- **Tone**: Informational but personal, community-supportive, sometimes darkly humorous about the relentlessness of diabetes management
- **Quality note**: Academic research (2025, American Diabetes Association journal) found diabetes TikTok content is understandable but often lacks actionable clinical accuracy -- opportunity for credible partners

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **@clintonsvatos** -- 1.5M followers. One of the largest diabetes creators on TikTok
2. **@yoowaddupbruh** -- 170.1K followers. Type 1 diabetes daily life content
3. **@audrey..dickson** -- 166.4K followers. T1D lifestyle and management
4. **@sierraortiz3** -- 120.6K followers. Diabetes education and daily management
5. **Dr. Yumi** -- Notable for diabetes-friendly recipe content

### Meal Planning Relevance
**VERY HIGH.** Meal planning is arguably the single most discussed practical topic in DiabetesTok:
- Every meal requires carb counting, glycemic impact assessment, and insulin timing
- "What I eat in a day" is the dominant content format, inherently tied to meal planning
- Meal prep specifically for blood sugar stability is a dedicated content niche
- TikTok features a dedicated channel for "Food for Diabetics" curated by the platform
- Diabetes meal planner books are actively discussed and recommended

### Competitive Presence
- **HIGH competition from diabetes-specific apps**: mySugr (bolus calculator, carb counting, CGM sync), Fooducate (barcode scanning, food grading), and numerous blood sugar tracking apps
- **Tandem Diabetes Care** actively sponsors TikTok creators for device promotion
- **Device companies** (Dexcom, Libre, Omnipod) are major brand presences
- **Meal planning apps** are less dominant -- most diabetes apps focus on tracking rather than planning
- Gap: no dominant app specifically generates personalized meal PLANS optimized for blood sugar management

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: HVP parents of diabetic children are prime targets. Peacekeeper applies when one family member's diabetes requires the entire family meal system to accommodate
- **Key feature angle**: Meal plans that account for glycemic considerations across the whole family. 16 dietary restrictions capability is directly relevant. Grocery list generation reduces the cognitive load of carb-counting every ingredient
- **Positioning**: "A weekly meal plan your whole family can eat -- built around the dietary needs that matter most." Emphasize that Slated doesn't just track; it PLANS
- **Entry angle**: Partner with mid-tier diabetes creators who focus on practical daily management (not just device reviews). The "meal plan" → "blood sugar stability" pipeline is clinically meaningful and emotionally resonant
- **Caution**: This community is medically sophisticated. Claims must be accurate. Consider consulting with a diabetes educator before creating content

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Saves are EXTREMELY high on meal-related content (recipes, meal prep guides, "what I eat" compilations)
- Comments are information-dense: people share their own blood sugar responses to specific foods
- Shares occur on diagnosis stories and relatable burnout content
- This is a "need-to-know" community -- practical content dramatically outperforms awareness content

## 4. #CeliacTok / #GlutenFreeTok

### Community Name & Key Hashtags
- Primary: #CeliacTok, #GlutenFreeTok, #GlutenFree, #CeliacDisease
- Specific: #GlutenFreeRecipes, #GlutenFreeLife, #GlutenFreeMealPrep, #GlutenFreeBread, #GlutenFreeDessert, #GlutenFreeTikTok
- Adjacent: #DairyFree, #FoodSensitivity, #GutHealth

### Size
- **#GlutenFree**: 7.3 billion overall views, 700.9K+ posts
- Over 591 gluten-free influencers tracked on Collabstr alone
- Social Cat identifies 30+ top gluten-free creators available for brand collaborations, drawn from a network of 100,000+ nutrition influencers
- TikTok features dedicated curated channels: "Gluten Free Meals" and "Gluten Free Food"

### Core Audience & Demographics
- **Celiac disease patients**: The medical-necessity segment. Every meal must be verified gluten-free. Estimated 1% of population (3.3M Americans), but awareness and diagnosis rates are rising
- **Gluten sensitivity / lifestyle choosers**: Larger but less urgent segment
- Skews female, ages 20-40
- Significant parent audience (parents managing celiac children's diets)
- "The gluten-free lifestyle can feel isolating, as it impacts grocery shopping, dining at restaurants, attending parties, and travel" -- community provides belonging

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: What I eat in a day (gluten-free), product reviews/ratings, restaurant reviews for safety, travel with celiac, gluten-free baking, "Is this gluten-free?" ingredient investigations, cross-contamination awareness
- **What performs well**: Product discovery (new GF products), restaurant/travel safety content, "I got glutened" stories (high engagement due to shared frustration), baking successes, grocery hauls
- **Tone**: Practical, supportive, occasionally frustrated with lack of accommodation. Mix of educational and lifestyle content
- **Unique dynamic**: High trust requirements -- creators must demonstrate genuine celiac knowledge. "Lifestyle" gluten-free creators are viewed with some suspicion by the celiac community

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **Phil (@philhatesgluten)** -- 220K TikTok followers. Male GF creator with EoE, HH, and GERD. Relatable and humorous
2. **Madison** -- 160.6K followers. Celiac + gut health content, workout routines, what to do if exposed to gluten
3. **Gabby** -- 120.1K followers. Celiac journey, "what I eat in a day," GF product ratings
4. **Lauren (@eatglutenfreewithme)** -- 85K followers. Celiac education, travel tips, finding GF-friendly food
5. **Lauren** (celiac/IBS/Crohn's) -- 70K followers. Multi-condition gut health content, living with bloating and gut issues
6. **Feelin' Fab With Kayla** -- Certified holistic nutritionist specializing in gluten-free meal prep content

### Meal Planning Relevance
**VERY HIGH.** Every single meal is a planning exercise for celiac patients:
- Cross-contamination risk means every ingredient must be verified
- Family meals where only one member has celiac create planning complexity
- Travel and social eating require advance planning
- Meal prep is a dedicated and popular content niche -- batch cooking safe meals for the week
- Grocery list management is critical (checking labels, finding safe brands)

### Competitive Presence
- **Fig App**: Active TikTok presence. Scans barcodes to verify food safety for specific dietary restrictions. Promoted by allergists on TikTok (e.g., @rubin_allergy)
- **Spokin App**: Free app with allergen-verified restaurant/food database. Active TikTok community engagement, curates "favorite food allergy and celiac TikTokers" lists. Has brand partnership program
- **Nima Sensor**: Gluten-testing device with TikTok presence
- **General apps**: Mealime (GF filter available), Fooducate
- Gap: No dominant app generates full WEEKLY meal plans for celiac families. Existing tools focus on scanning/verification, not planning

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: Peacekeeper is the dominant persona. The person managing a celiac family member's diet carries enormous invisible labor -- reading every label, calling restaurants, planning every meal around safety. "Your family's input before dinner. Not their complaints after."
- **Key feature angle**: Slated handles dietary restrictions as a core feature. A celiac-filtered weekly meal plan with auto-generated grocery list eliminates the per-meal ingredient verification burden
- **Positioning**: "Every meal, verified. Every week, planned." Emphasize that Slated's restriction handling means the plan is safe by default -- no checking required
- **Entry angle**: Partner with celiac-focused creators like Gabby or Lauren who already discuss the exhaustion of planning every meal. Demonstrate Slated generating a full GF week of dinners in seconds
- **Competitive differentiation vs. Fig/Spokin**: Those apps help you CHECK if a food is safe. Slated PLANS safe meals proactively. Complementary, not competitive

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Saves are the dominant engagement metric -- people save recipes, product recommendations, and restaurant lists for future reference
- Comments are often questions ("Is [brand] safe?" "Does this have cross-contamination risk?")
- High share rates on "I got glutened" stories and new product discoveries
- Trust is paramount -- one inaccurate claim can destroy credibility permanently in this community

- Primary: #FoodAllergyMom, #AllergyFriendly, #FoodAllergies, #FoodAllergy

- Feedspot tracks 40+ food allergy influencers

### Core Audience & Demographics
- **Primary**: Mothers managing children with multiple food allergies (the "allergy mom" identity is strong and self-proclaimed)
- **Secondary**: Adults with food allergies navigating daily life, dating, social situations
- Overwhelmingly female (mothers), ages 28-45
- Higher income skew (allergy-safe products cost more; this audience pays premium prices routinely)
- Emotionally intense -- food allergies in children are life-threatening, creating hypervigilance around every meal

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: Allergy-safe recipes, grocery hauls showing safe products, school lunch packing, restaurant experiences, anaphylaxis awareness, allergy-safe party food, advocacy for label laws, emotional labor of allergen management
- **What performs well**: "What I pack for my allergic child" lunch/snack content; product discoveries ("this is safe for Top 9!"); relatable mom humor about the vigilance required; scary-then-educational near-miss stories
- **Tone**: Protective, community-oriented, often emotionally charged. Humor as coping mechanism. Strong advocacy streak. Gatekeeping quality (non-allergy parents who "just don't get it" are a recurring theme)
- **Mother-daughter duo content** is a notable format -- moms and allergic kids creating together

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **Dr. Amiirah Aujnarain** (@doctoramiirah) -- 70K+ followers. Pediatrician creating allergy education content
2. **Megan Lavin** -- 23K+ followers. Dairy-free and gluten-free family meals, parent of allergic child
3. **Mia (@theallergicgirl)** -- Growing following. Daily life with 50+ food allergies, grocery shopping process, skincare routines
4. **@sarahandspices** -- Food allergy navigation in social contexts (dating, dinner parties, friendships)

6. **Food Allergy Diva** (@foodallergydiva) -- Recipes and allergy-friendly living

### Meal Planning Relevance
**EXTREMELY HIGH.** This is arguably the community where meal planning is most acutely necessary:
- Managing multiple allergens across multiple family members (e.g., one child is nut-free, another is dairy-free, a third eats everything)
- Every meal must be safe -- there is zero margin for error (anaphylaxis risk)
- School lunches, family dinners, birthday parties, and holiday meals all require advance planning
- Grocery shopping is a multi-hour label-reading exercise
- "What do I make for dinner that EVERYONE can eat?" is the defining daily question

### Competitive Presence
- **Spokin App**: The most established food allergy app with TikTok presence. Free. Features verified restaurants, products, and brand partnerships. Ambassador program with 20-30 influencer relationships. Has a "For Food Brands" marketing platform

- **Brands**: Enjoy Life Foods (allergen-free snack brand) partners with Spokin. "Free-from" food brands are active sponsors in this space

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: Peacekeeper is the PERFECT fit. The allergy mom IS the Peacekeeper -- she carries the entire family food system, manages the invisible labor of keeping everyone safe and fed, and absorbs the emotional weight of food decisions. "Your family's input before dinner. Not their complaints after." is almost tailor-made for this audience
- **Key feature angle**: 12 allergy types handled natively. Slated generates a weekly plan where EVERY meal is safe for EVERY family member's specific allergens. Grocery list is pre-filtered for safety. Dinner Draft lets family members weigh in on preferences without compromising safety constraints
- **Positioning**: "One plan. Every allergen handled. Every family member fed." or "Stop reading labels for an hour every Sunday. Let the plan do it."
- **Entry angle**: This community is HIGHLY receptive to products that reduce their burden. Partner with mid-tier allergy moms (10K-50K range) who do "what I feed my allergic family" content. Demonstrate Slated building a week of dinners for a family with 3 different allergen profiles
- **HIGH PRIORITY COMMUNITY**: The Peacekeeper persona alignment, medical necessity of meal planning, willingness to pay for convenience, and competitive gap (no existing app does weekly multi-allergen family meal planning) make this potentially the highest-ROI community for Slated

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Saves are EXTREMELY high -- allergy-safe recipes and product recommendations are bookmarked religiously
- Shares are high on awareness and advocacy content
- Comments are often urgent questions and personal stories
- This community will pay for solutions -- they already pay premium prices for safe food
- Trust and accuracy are literally life-or-death. One mistake (recommending a food with a hidden allergen) would be catastrophic for brand reputation

## 6. #ARFID / #SensoryProcessingTok

### Community Name & Key Hashtags
- Primary: #ARFID, #ARFIDRecovery, #ARFIDAwareness, #SensoryProcessingTok
- Specific: #OneBiteChallenge, #SafeFoods, #FoodExposure, #MyARFIDLife, #ARFIDAndreww
- Adjacent: #EatingDisorderRecovery, #EDAW, #SelectiveEating, #FoodAnxiety
- Overlapping: #AutismTok, #SensoryProcessing (significant overlap with Community #1)

### Size
- **Smaller than other communities in this report** but rapidly growing due to awareness campaigns
- #ARFID hashtag: Specific view counts not publicly confirmed, but the #OneBiteChallenge went viral and drove significant traffic
- #ARFIDRecovery is part of a broader eating disorder recovery hashtag ecosystem studied academically
- Key creator @myarfidlife (Hannah Lee) has 640K+ followers -- a single creator with that following indicates substantial community size
- "ARFID Andrew" (Andrew Luber) gained 200K+ followers since June 2024, showing rapid growth trajectory

### Core Audience & Demographics
- **People with ARFID**: Children, teens, and adults with clinically restrictive eating patterns
- **Parents of children with ARFID**: Major audience segment, often the most distressed
- **Overlap with autism community**: ARFID and autism frequently co-occur; significant audience crossover
- Younger skew (teens and young adults), but parent audience is 30-45
- Gender diverse; ARFID affects all genders relatively equally
- High correlation with anxiety, OCD, and autism spectrum conditions

### Content Culture
- **Dominant themes**: Food exposure journeys (trying new foods on camera), safe food documentation, awareness/education about what ARFID IS (many people have never heard of it), recovery narratives, "what I eat in a day with ARFID," debunking "just eat" dismissals
- **What performs well**: The #OneBiteChallenge (started by 8-year-old Hannah Lea to spread ARFID awareness), food exposure victories ("I tried X for the first time!"), educational explainers distinguishing ARFID from picky eating, safe food compilations
- **Tone**: Vulnerable, courageous, educational. Heavy emphasis on "this is a real disorder, not pickiness." Recovery-oriented but not pushy. Sensory language is prominent
- **Unique dynamic**: This community is still in an awareness-building phase. Many viewers are discovering ARFID for the first time through this content

### Key Creators (with approximate followers)
1. **Hannah Lee (@myarfidlife)** -- 640K+ followers. 8-year-old with ARFID who started the #OneBiteChallenge. Shows what life with ARFID really looks like. Parent-managed account
2. **Andrew Luber ("ARFID Andrew")** -- 200K+ followers. Gained following since June 2024 through food exposure content and advocacy for exposure therapy. Spoke at universities about ARFID
3. Other creators document personal ARFID journeys but with smaller followings (typically under 50K)
4. Eating disorder recovery therapists who include ARFID content

### Meal Planning Relevance
**HIGH but SPECIALIZED.** Meal planning for ARFID is fundamentally different:
- The challenge isn't "what to make" but "what CAN they eat" -- safe food lists may be 5-15 items total
- Predictability is essential: foods that always look, taste, feel, and smell the same (e.g., goldfish crackers) are preferred over variable foods (e.g., strawberries)
- Meal planning reduces the anxiety of unknown meals
- Parents need to plan meals around safe foods while gradually introducing exposure opportunities
- Nutritional adequacy with extremely limited food acceptance is a medical concern

### Competitive Presence
- **Very low.** This is an underserved niche
- No meal planning apps specifically target ARFID
- Eating disorder recovery apps (Recovery Record, Rise Up) focus on tracking and therapy support, not meal planning
- Occupational therapists and feeding therapists are the primary professional presence
- Brands are largely absent -- this community is too small for most food companies to target directly

### Slated Fit Assessment
- **Persona**: Peacekeeper, specifically the parent variant. The parent of a child with ARFID carries extreme emotional labor around feeding -- planning every meal around a tiny safe food list, managing siblings' different needs, worrying about nutrition
- **Key feature angle**: Dietary restriction handling (16 restrictions) applied to ARFID-level specificity. The ability to build a meal plan from a constrained ingredient list. Dinner Draft could help ARFID families plan meals that include safe foods for the affected member alongside broader options for others
- **Positioning**: "Plans that work with what your family can actually eat." Validate the restriction rather than suggesting they need to expand it
- **Entry angle**: This is a NICHE play, not a volume play. Partner with ARFID parent advocates or feeding therapists. Small, targeted content showing Slated building a meal plan around a very limited safe food list
- **Sensitivity required**: ARFID is a clinical eating disorder. Marketing must be careful not to trivialize or suggest the app replaces professional treatment. Position Slated as a practical support tool alongside therapy

### Engagement Quality Notes
- Very high save rates on safe food lists and educational content
- Comments are deeply emotional -- parents sharing relief at finding community
- Shares on awareness content are high (people sharing to educate their families/friends about ARFID)
- Smaller community means less volume but VERY high engagement depth per post
- Sensitivity around eating disorder content means TikTok may restrict some content distribution

### Meal Planning Relevance
**EXTREMELY HIGH.** Energy management makes meal planning medically necessary:
- "Spoon theory" means every activity costs energy. Cooking dinner might be the ONLY thing someone can do that evening
- Pre-planning meals reduces decision energy AND physical energy spent figuring things out in the moment
- Batch cooking on "good days" to have meals ready for "bad days" is a core strategy
- Grocery shopping is physically exhausting -- a pre-made list (and grocery delivery) is essential
- "Low spoon meals" is an active content niche: 3-ingredient dinners, no-chop recipes, dump-and-go slow cooker meals
- The Gourmet Spoonie specifically documents how they plan, shop, and cook with chronic illness -- demonstrating that this audience already thinks in meal planning terms
- Strategies include: looking for recipes with few ingredients, reading methods in advance to assess energy requirements, and checking for labor-intensive stages

### Competitive Presence
- **Low from meal planning apps specifically.** General apps (Mealime, Paprika) don't account for energy/capacity constraints
- **Grocery delivery services** (Instacart, Amazon Fresh) are popular by necessity
- **Meal kit services** have some presence but are often too energy-intensive (still requires cooking)
- **Adaptive kitchen tools** brands (one-handed can openers, lightweight cookware) advertise in this space
- Gap: No meal planning app factors in energy/capacity as a planning variable. "Low spoon" meal filtering is unaddressed by any app

### Key Themes Across All Communities

1. **Decision fatigue is universal.** Every community experiences the "what's for dinner?" paralysis, but for different reasons (sensory overwhelm, medical necessity, cognitive overload, energy depletion, safety fear).

2. **"Saves" dominate engagement in medical/dietary communities.** This indicates content with lasting utility value -- exactly what Slated would provide (meal plans, grocery lists).

3. **The planning gap is real.** Existing food tech in these spaces focuses on TRACKING (blood sugar, allergens, safe foods) or CHECKING (barcode scanning, restaurant verification). Almost nobody generates proactive WEEKLY PLANS. This is Slated's differentiation.

4. **Trust is non-negotiable.** Every community values accuracy and authenticity above all. Slated cannot afford a single incorrect allergen, a recipe with hidden gluten, or a suggestion that ignores sensory constraints. The restriction-handling feature set must be bulletproof before entering these communities.

5. **Willingness to pay is high.** These audiences already pay premiums for safety, convenience, and accommodation. $7.99/month is within range for all communities except potentially the most budget-constrained chronic illness audiences.

6. **Creator partnerships > traditional advertising.** Every community trusts peer voices over brand messaging. Mid-tier creators (50K-200K followers) in each niche are the optimal partnership target: large enough for reach, small enough for authenticity.

### Feature Prioritization Recommendations

Based on this research, the following Slated features are most critical for these audiences:

1. **Multi-allergen family profiles**: Different restrictions for each family member, unified weekly plan
2. **Effort/energy level filtering**: "Low spoon," "minimal prep," "one-pot" filters for chronic illness audience
3. **Sensory preference handling**: Texture, temperature, and ingredient familiarity settings for autism/ARFID audiences
4. **Grocery list with delivery integration**: One-click order from the plan for energy-limited users
5. **Dinner Draft with safety constraints**: Family voting that operates within allergen/dietary safety boundaries

## Exclusion Persona Check

None of these 7 communities are dominated by the exclusion personas:

- **Budget Optimizer** ("I will work harder to save $5"): While chronic illness audiences may have income constraints, their motivation is ENERGY savings, not cost savings. Food allergy audiences pay premium prices willingly. No community centers frugality as a core value.
- **Perfectionist** ("I will work harder for better food"): These communities optimize for SAFETY, FEASIBILITY, and REDUCED BURDEN -- not culinary excellence. Nobody in #FoodAllergyMom is trying to make a Michelin-star dinner; they're trying to make sure nobody goes into anaphylaxis.

All 7 communities align with the Slated value proposition of reducing cognitive load and emotional labor around dinner planning.
