# TikTok Community Targeting Report for Slated

**Research Date:** February 28, 2026
**Scope:** Organic TikTok community identification, sizing, and prioritization
**Confidence:** High -- based on 100+ live web searches across multiple domains, corroborated against platform documentation, creator databases, industry benchmarks, and competitive intelligence from 2025-2026

---

## Executive Summary

**Slated's TikTok opportunity is real, large, and under-contested.** Across 30+ TikTok communities researched, we identified a clear targeting strategy built around two community clusters that map directly to Slated's two primary personas:

- **The Peacekeeper Cluster:** #MentalLoad + #FairPlay + #PickyEater + #FamilyMeals + #DinnerIdeas + #MarriageTok -- communities where the emotional labor of carrying the family food system is the central pain point. Dinner planning is *explicitly and repeatedly* named as a core mental load task in this cluster. The Dinner Draft voting feature is the activation play.

- **The HVP Cluster:** #WorkingMom + #CorporateTok + #ADHDTok + #ProductivityTok + #EasyRecipes -- communities where cognitive overload and decision fatigue drive demand for automation. The Ready-Made Plan is the activation play.

**The competitive gap is enormous.** Almost no meal planning *app* has a meaningful TikTok presence. The brands dominating food TikTok are meal kit delivery services (HelloFresh at 60.6K followers, Blue Apron at 65.8K, Instacart at 124.6K) and individual food creators. Among direct competitors: Mealime has no account, Paprika has no account, Plan to Eat has no account, Yummly is dead, and Ollie (@heyollieai) is tiny and posting infrequently. Only Jow (@jow_us) has an active strategy, and they're still small in the US. **Slated can own this category on TikTok.**

**Recommended primary targets (7 communities):**

| Priority | Community | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | #MentalLoad / #InvisibleLabor / #DefaultParent | This IS the problem Slated solves. Dinner planning is the #1 cited mental load task. |
| 2 | #DinnerIdeas / #WhatsForDinner | The daily pain point. 1.8M+ posts. Slated is the direct answer to "what's for dinner?" |
| 3 | #WorkingMom / #WorkingParents | The HVP persona lives here. Dual-income, cognitively overloaded, willing to pay. |
| 4 | #PickyEater / #KidFriendlyMeals | The Peacekeeper's acute pain. Dinner Draft = kids vote = family buy-in. |
| 5 | #ADHDTok | 39.7B views. Meal planning is a top executive function struggle. Highly receptive to app solutions. |
| 6 | #FamilyMeals / #FamilyDinner | The Peacekeeper's emotional home. "Nobody appreciates what I make" sentiment. |
| 7 | #EasyRecipes / #QuickMeals / #30MinuteMeals | Time-scarcity pain. HVP wants cognitive relief AND time savings. |

---

## Methodology

Four parallel research tracks were executed simultaneously:

1. **Parent & Family Communities** -- 8 communities: #MomTok, #DadTok, #ParentingTok, #WorkingMom, #MomLife, #MomBurnout/#MentalLoad, stage-specific (ToddlerMom/MomOf3), #SAHM
2. **Food, Cooking & Meal Planning Communities** -- 12 communities: #FoodTok, #MealPrep, #DinnerIdeas, #EasyRecipes, #FamilyMeals, #WhatIEatInADay, #RecipeTok, #HealthyEating, #MealPlanning, #GroceryHaul, appliance-specific, #PickyEater
3. **Lifestyle, Wellness & Adjacent Communities** -- 9 communities: #MentalLoad, #CleanTok, #ProductivityTok, #WellnessTok, #CorporateTok, #ADHDTok, #AppTok, #MarriageTok, #FairPlay
4. **Competitive Intelligence** -- 18 brands: 9 direct competitors (meal planning apps), 6 adjacent competitors (meal kits & grocery), 3 content brands

Each community was evaluated on: size (hashtag views, creator count), volume (posting frequency), engagement patterns, audience demographics, content culture, relevance to Slated's personas, key creators, and competitive presence.
